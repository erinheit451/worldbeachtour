## 2. What makes a signature page work

Distilled from what landed on Copa and Brighton, and what stayed thin on early drafts:

1. **Cultural layering.** A good signature has books, films, songs, named people, political history, architectural landmarks — not just beach geometry. Copa has 18 cultural refs; Brighton has 18. Pages that only have geography (e.g., "remote paradise") render thin because the text has nowhere to go.

2. **Contested / honest context.** The Favela Above on Copa, Kemptown on Brighton, the Aboriginal country on Bondi. A page that elides the uncomfortable part of its place feels like a postcard. The best pages have something to apologize for, or not.

3. **Dense Wikipedia trail.** Pages where every second claim has a Wikipedia link feel authoritative. The research-heavy beaches (Brighton, Copa) cited 30+ Wikipedia articles; the thin ones had nothing to hang on.

4. **Commons photo availability.** Beaches with 10+ free-licensed photos across multiple eras and angles produce layered pages. Beaches with 2-3 photos produce thin ones. Instagram-famous beaches are often *worst* for free imagery because everything's on Instagram, not Commons.

5. **A distinct voice register.** Copa is energetic/maximalist; Brighton is grey and rueful; Waikīkī is reverent; Nazaré is technical/sport; Glass Beach is geological/melancholic. Duplicating a register adds less than adding a new one.

## 4. Candidate pool (ranked with DB evidence)

Notability scores / Wikipedia pageviews queried from our existing DB. Blank = enrichment gap (real content exists but our pipeline didn't link it).

### Tier A — ship next

**1. Coney Island, Brooklyn (USA)** · `coney-island-beach-1` · notab **44.3**, **424K views**
- **Why it's a signature:** the most-viewed beach in our DB except Copa. Birthplace of the rollercoaster, hot dog (Nathan's 1916), the American amusement-park idiom. Appears in *Annie Hall*, *The Warriors*, *Requiem for a Dream*, *Lords of Dogtown*. The Cyclone (1927) is still running. Mermaid Parade every June. The sideshow, the freak shows, the specifically *American* low-brow seaside that Brighton has a mirror-image British version of.
- **Register it adds:** urban American, historical amusement-park culture, the seaside as working-class democracy
- **Content depth:** enormous. 100+ Wikipedia articles. Dense film/literature trail. Complete rise → near-death → modern-revival arc.
- **Commons:** rich, including PD archival photos from the 1900s-50s.
- **Risk:** none. This one is overdetermined.

**2. Maya Bay, Thailand** · `haad-aawmaahyaan-y` · enrichment gap (no wiki link yet)
- **Why it's a signature:** the conservation-reopening story is THE modern beach story. Featured in *The Beach* (Leonardo DiCaprio, 2000), then loved to death — closed entirely in 2018 after ecological collapse, reopened 2022 with strict visitor caps. A parable about mass tourism that no other beach tells this sharply.
- **Register it adds:** Asia coverage + ecotourism / conservation tension (the biggest missing register)
- **Content depth:** high — the cinema story, the ecological papers, the government policy, the ongoing debate
- **Commons:** medium. Plenty of reef + bay shots. Less of the pre-shutdown crowd imagery (rights issues).
- **Risk:** needs sensitive framing. The beach's meaning IS the tension.

**3. Reynisfjara (Iceland) — or Papakōlea green sand (Hawaii)** · both enrichment gaps
- **Why a signature:** unique geology as the entire pitch. Reynisfjara has black sand, basalt columns, rogue waves that kill tourists most years — a cautionary dark-tourism register. Papakōlea has green olivine sand, one of 4 green sand beaches on Earth, located on Hawaiian sovereign-rights contested land.
- **Register it adds:** geological oddity beyond Glass Beach
- **My pick of the two: Reynisfjara.** More iconic, better Commons coverage, cleaner narrative arc (basalt → tourism → drowning deaths → Iceland's weather warning system).

## 7. Process notes for whoever builds these

The Copa/Brighton build pattern that worked:

1. **Research dossier agent first.** 600–1200 words, 30+ sources, every claim with a URL. Cultural/historical facts, specific named people, verified dates. Catches folklore before it enters the prose.
2. **Photo sourcing agent second.** 14–18 verified Commons URLs across hero + section images + timeline events + landmarks. Flags what's missing (e.g., "no PD tanga-archival on Commons").
3. **DB enrichment script third.** intro_text (~900w), context note, day_in_time, food_drink, 15-20 timeline events, 5-8 zones, 4-6 landmarks, 15-18 cultural_refs, 5-6 recurring_events, 5-6 businesses. Write to `content/beaches/<slug>/showcase.json` sidecar so the pipeline can't overwrite it.
4. **LegendaryBeach page.tsx fourth.** Pass the sidecar through, add 1–3 signature components (per-beach unique), set the versus comparison, the stay zones, the planner rows.
5. **Add slug to `SHOWCASE_SLUGS`** in `app/beaches/[slug]/layout.tsx` so the dynamic route doesn't overwrite.
6. **Signature custom component(s)** — this is where the beach's specificity lives. Copa's Calçadão, Brighton's Piers, Nazaré's Canyon/McNamara/Village/Paths.
7. **Build + deploy.**
8. **Iterate 3-4 feedback rounds** — the first ship is rarely great. The Copa pattern took 5 rounds of "what's thin, what's redundant, what photos are missing."

**Budget:** 4–6 hours per signature for a first draft. Another 2–3 hours of polish across iterations.

## 8. What NOT to do

- **Don't add a fourth surf beach.** We have Waikiki, Bondi, Nazaré. A fourth (Jeffreys, Pipeline, Bells) would over-represent surf.
- **Don't add a second Brazilian beach.** Sancho, Jericoacoara, Ipanema all tempt — but Copa already carries Brazil's cultural weight in the English-speaking imagination.
- **Don't add a pure-paradise beach without a cultural angle.** Pink Sands Bahamas, Anse Source d'Argent are beautiful but the page would be thin. These belong in Tier 2 with enriched photos + data, not Tier 1 signature treatment.
- **Don't chase notability score alone.** Praia da Marinha scores 40.8 but its content ceiling is lower than Maya Bay at 2.5. Score is a useful signal, not a ranking.
