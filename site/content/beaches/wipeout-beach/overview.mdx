Wipeout Beach, formally known as Children's Pool Beach, is a defining feature of La Jolla's rugged coastal landscape, situated just 0.5 kilometers south of the village proper. Located at the intersection of Prospect Street and Coast Boulevard, this iconic cove sits at 32.845°N, 117.279°W on the edge of San Diego County's most densely developed coastal zone. The beach has become inseparable from La Jolla's identity—not as a swimming destination, but as a wildlife sanctuary and photographic landmark that captures the raw California coast.

Unlike the flat sandy beaches of central San Diego, Wipeout is defined by its dramatic geology: rocky intertidal zones interspersed with small sandy patches, bounded by the distinctive Children's Pool seawall built in 1931. The pool itself—the curved concrete structure visible from Prospect Street—was originally designed as a protected swimming area for families. Today it serves an entirely different purpose: as a marine mammal haul-out, where California sea lions and harbor seals congregate, rest, and breed. The beach itself remains public, but the pool's designation as a seal preserve has redefined visitor expectations and access patterns fundamentally.

What makes Wipeout remarkable is the sheer biological density of this small cove. The beach supports 20 observed species across mammals, birds, and invertebrates, with California sea lions (7,288 observations) and Brandt's cormorants (3,759 observations) dominating the landscape. The intertidal zone itself is a living laboratory: hermissend opalescentid nudibranchs (2,123 observations), porcelain crabs (1,219), and colorful sea anemones create a textured ecosystem that changes dramatically with the tidal cycle. Brown pelicans, now recovered from near-extinction, hunt constantly in the shallow waters offshore. This is functional marine habitat, not a manicured attraction.

**Most Relevant Lenses:**

**Wildlife & Marine Biology** — Wipeout is fundamentally a marine observation site. The seal and sea lion populations are significant enough to warrant protected status, and the intertidal ecology offers genuine scientific interest. This is where you come to understand San Diego's coastal ecosystem, not to recreate on sand.

**Photography & Landscape** — The rocky cove, stacked cliffs, dramatic light angles, and the juxtaposition of fur seals against concrete architecture create one of San Diego County's most photographed coastal vistas. Golden hour here is legendary.

**History & Culture** — The Children's Pool seawall and the shift from human swimming to marine mammal sanctuary tells a 95-year story of coastal philosophy evolution in California. The designation reflects genuine conservation success.

**Accessibility & Accessibility Limits** — Unlike other La Jolla coves, Wipeout has dedicated restroom facilities and paved access from Prospect Street. It's genuinely accessible for families and elderly visitors, yet the seal preserve boundary creates clear behavioral limits. This tension is worth understanding.

Wipeout Beach succeeds because it refuses compromise: it is simultaneously accessible and wild, human-scaled and ecologically serious, urban and genuinely marine. Visitors come not to use the beach but to witness it.
