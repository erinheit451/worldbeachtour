# Playa La Tejita Travel Guide

Playa La Tejita is one of Tenerife's few remaining undeveloped beaches, a 1-kilometer stretch of black volcanic sand located directly beneath Montaña Roja's cinder cone. Situated in the municipality of El Médano just 2 kilometers from Tenerife Sur Airport (TFS), La Tejita occupies a unique position in the Canary Islands landscape—it's accessible enough for a quick visit between flights, yet wild enough to feel like genuine escape from tourist infrastructure. The beach is particularly known among travelers seeking clothing-optional zones and for its reputation as a premier kitesurf destination during seasonal trade winds.

## Getting There

### By Air

Most international visitors arrive at **Tenerife Sur Airport (TFS)**, one of Spain's busiest regional hubs. La Tejita is exceptionally close—just 2.2 kilometers away, making it roughly a 5-minute drive from baggage claim. This proximity makes La Tejita unique among Canarian beaches: you can land, rent a vehicle, and be on sand within 30 minutes.

Car rental agencies operate from all three terminals at TFS. Standard vehicle rental costs range from €25–40 per day for economy compact cars, with unlimited mileage standard on most contracts. International driver's license requirements vary by nationality; US and EU licenses are accepted without formal international permits.

### By Car

From TFS Airport, take the **TF-1 highway** north toward El Médano (clearly signed). The drive to La Tejita takes approximately 10–15 minutes depending on traffic and parking location. The TF-1 is a well-maintained dual carriageway with clear signage toward El Médano town.

From the town of El Médano itself, La Tejita is accessible via local roads; follow signage toward the beach (Playa La Tejita). Parking is free and widely available along the access roads to the beach. There is no formal parking lot, but roadside parking extends along the approach routes.

If driving from other parts of Tenerife:
- **From Puerto de la Cruz (North)** (100 km): Approximately 1.5–2 hours via TF-5 and TF-1
- **From Los Cristianos (Southwest)** (30 km): Approximately 45 minutes to 1 hour via TF-1
- **From Santa Cruz (Northeast)** (85 km): Approximately 1.5 hours via TF-5 and TF-1

### By Public Transit

Bus service connects Tenerife Sur Airport to El Médano town via **TITSA** (Tenerife's public transport operator). The journey takes approximately 30–40 minutes. From El Médano town center, local buses or taxis provide final-mile access to La Tejita (approximately 2 kilometers). Bus fares are modest—typically €3–5 for airport-to-town routes.

Once in El Médano, the town is walkable, and signage toward Playa La Tejita is available. The beach is technically walkable from town (roughly 3–4 kilometers along the coast), though the coastal path is not formally developed.

## Best Time to Visit

### Seasonal Overview

**Year-Round Accessibility** — Unlike many Spanish beaches, Playa La Tejita is genuinely visitable in all seasons due to Tenerife's stable subtropical climate. Temperatures remain in the 60–75°F (15–24°C) range even in winter months. This consistency reflects the stabilizing influence of the Atlantic Ocean's thermal inertia.

**Winter (November–March)** brings the most consistent trade winds, making this the prime season for kitesurf and windsport enthusiasts. Daytime temperatures range from 60–68°F (15–20°C). Rainfall is minimal even in winter—Tenerife's southern coast is in a rain shadow created by the island's volcanic interior. Ocean temperatures dip to approximately 62°F (17°C), manageable with wetsuits. Crowds are minimal, offering the most authentic experience of the beach's wild character.

**Spring (April–May)** offers warming conditions (66–72°F/19–22°C) with still-consistent trade winds. Ocean temperatures begin warming toward 64°F (18°C). This shoulder season balances comfortable conditions with moderate tourist activity.

**Summer (June–August)** sees peak temperatures (73–82°F/23–28°C), though the trade winds moderate what could otherwise feel oppressive. Ocean temperatures reach approximately 72°F (22°C)—the warmest of the year, though still requiring consideration for extended swimming. Summer brings the largest crowds to El Médano's facilities, though La Tejita itself remains relatively quiet due to its lack of commercial amenities. Sun exposure is intense; UV protection is essential year-round in the Canary Islands.

**Fall (September–October)** mirrors spring conditions with gradual cooling (70–75°F/21–24°C) and declining visitor numbers. Trade winds remain consistent, maintaining excellent conditions for watersports.

### Practical Seasonal Note

The trade winds that define Tenerife's southern coast are most reliable from November through March, peaking in winter months. These winds can exceed 20 knots, creating both excellent conditions for kitesurf and challenging conditions for other beach activities. Hat retention becomes a practical concern; secure any loose gear.

## Where to Stay

### El Médano Town (Adjacent)

El Médano, immediately north of La Tejita, is the closest accommodation option. This working kitesurf town has developed modest tourism infrastructure: small hotels, guesthouses (casas rurales), and apartment rentals. Most El Médano accommodations are within 2–5 kilometers of La Tejita. Hotel rates typically range from €60–100 per night for mid-range properties. The town itself has restaurants, supermarkets, and small shops catering to the kitesurf community.

### Los Abrigos (8 km)

This fishing village immediately east of El Médano is known for its authentic fish restaurants operating directly from the harbor. Accommodation here is similar to El Médano: modest guesthouses and apartment rentals (€50–80 per night). Los Abrigos offers a more local, less tourism-focused atmosphere while remaining within 10–15 minutes' drive of La Tejita.

### Costa del Silencio (15 km)

This residential development south of El Médano offers apartment rentals and small hotels (€70–110 per night). The area has developed a reputation as a quieter alternative to more tourist-heavy Tenerife destinations. It's approximately 15–20 minutes' drive from La Tejita.

### Tenerife Sur Airport Adjacent (Luxury Alternative)

The **Playa Sur Tenerife** and similar 4-star properties operate near the airport itself (10–15 km south), catering to connecting passengers and those prioritizing proximity to transportation hubs. These range from €120–200+ per night.

## Costs & Budgeting

**Accommodation:** €50–100 per night for guest-house or modest hotel

**Food:** Restaurant meals in El Médano and Los Abrigos run €12–25 for casual dining. Fish restaurants in Los Abrigos are reasonably priced (€15–30). Supermarket groceries are available in El Médano at standard Spanish pricing.

**Transportation:** Car rental €25–40/day; fuel costs are typical for Spanish mainland prices (approximately €1.40–1.60/liter)

**Beach Activities:** La Tejita itself requires no fees. Kitesurf lesson packages through El Médano schools range from €100–150 for group sessions, €200+ for private instruction. Equipment rental is available through the kitesurf schools.

**Overall Daily Budget:** Budget €80–150 per person for a comfortable mid-range experience (accommodation, food, local transport).

## Safety Considerations

### Trade Winds & Sun Exposure

The trade winds that make La Tejita appealing to watersports enthusiasts also present real hazards for casual visitors. Winds routinely exceed 15 knots and can gust significantly higher. **Secure all loose items**—hats, phones, valuables. The winds are strongest in the afternoon and during winter months.

Sun exposure is intense year-round at this latitude (28°N). UV radiation levels are high even on cloudy days. **Apply high-SPF sunscreen (SPF 50+) and reapply every 2 hours** when in contact with water or sand. Bring a wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses, and consider rash guards for extended water time. Dehydration occurs rapidly in combination with wind and sun.

### Water Conditions

Water temperatures remain cool year-round. Even in summer, temperatures barely exceed 72°F (22°C). Wetsuits (3–5mm depending on season) are strongly recommended for swimming or water activities beyond paddling. The beach's volcanic black sand heats substantially under sun but retains heat poorly when wet—keep dry clothing nearby.

Ocean currents exist in this region, though La Tejita's configuration offers both exposed and sheltered areas. Always assess local conditions and consider the advice of locals or kitesurf schools regarding current status.

### Infrastructure

La Tejita has **no commercial facilities**—no lifeguards, no restrooms, no food vendors. Bring water, sun protection, and any supplies you'll need. The nearest facilities are in El Médano town (2 km). This is intentional and part of the beach's appeal; accept the trade-off between wildness and convenience.

### Crime & Security

The El Médano/Los Abrigos region is safe by Spanish standards. Vehicle break-ins do occur in tourist areas; do not leave valuables visible in parked vehicles. El Médano town operates normally during daylight hours. Petty theft is rare but not impossible in crowded periods.

## Tips & Highlights

### Montaña Roja Short Hike

The 574-meter cinder cone itself is accessible via marked hiking trails. The ascent typically requires 45–60 minutes round-trip depending on fitness and route selection. The summit offers 360-degree views across southern Tenerife, the neighboring islands, and across the Atlantic. Early morning starts are recommended to avoid afternoon sun exposure and wind strength. Bring substantial water and sun protection.

### El Médano Kitesurf Scene

If visiting during winter months, the El Médano waterfront becomes a hub for international kitesurf practitioners. Multiple schools offer lessons for beginners; this is one of Europe's premier kitesurf destinations. Equipment rental packages are available from numerous operators.

### Los Abrigos Fish Restaurants

This working fishing village has developed a reputation for authentic fish and seafood restaurants operating directly from the harbor. These establishments serve that morning's catch at reasonable prices. Establishments like the harbor-front restaurants offer €15–25 lunch specials featuring local fish. **Reserve ahead during summer weekends**; these spots attract locals and informed travelers.

### Timing for Solitude

La Tejita is genuinely quiet most of the year due to its lack of infrastructure. Visit during weekday mornings, off-season periods (April–May or September–October), or winter months to experience the beach at its most peaceful. Tourist crowds concentrate in El Médano town and at formal beaches further along the coast; La Tejita itself remains relatively untouched.

### Photography

The contrast between Montaña Roja's rust-red slopes and the black volcanic sand creates distinctive photographic opportunities. Early morning and late afternoon light angles are particularly striking. The beach's relative emptiness and dramatic geology offer compositional opportunities uncommon in developed tourist beach zones.
