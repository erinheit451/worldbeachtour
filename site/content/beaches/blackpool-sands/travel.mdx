# Blackpool Sands Travel Guide

Blackpool Sands sits on the Devon coast between Dartmouth and Kingsbridge, a short drive from the A379 coastal road. Despite its distance from major transport hubs, the beach rewards the small effort required to reach it—a privately managed cove where quality matters more than convenience. This guide covers getting there, timing your visit, and making the most of the surrounding South Hams landscape.

## Getting There

### By Air

Most visitors arrive through **Exeter International Airport (EXT)**, located 65 kilometers north of Blackpool Sands (approximately 1 hour 15 minutes drive). This is the primary international gateway for southwest England, with connections to London, European cities, and regional UK destinations.

**From Exeter to Blackpool Sands:**
- Exit the airport and follow signs toward Exeter city center
- Take the A30 south toward Okehampton/Tavistock
- Follow signs for Ashburton and Buckfastleigh (continuing on A38/A384)
- At Totnes, join the A381 toward Dartmouth
- Two kilometers before Dartmouth, turn left onto the A379 toward Stoke Fleming
- Follow signs to Blackpool Sands (clearly marked village parking)
- Total driving time: 1 hour 10–15 minutes in normal traffic

**Alternative: Plymouth (PLY)** — 75 kilometers away (1 hour 20 minutes), an older airport with fewer direct flights but occasionally cheaper fares. The route is similar: take the A386 to Ivybridge, then A38 south, connecting to A381 toward Totnes.

### By Train and Bus

**Train to Totnes** — The most practical rail option. Regular trains run from London Waterloo (2 hours 15 minutes), Manchester (4 hours), and other major cities to Totnes Station, a charming Victorian station in the town.

From Totnes Station:
- Take **Bus 3 (Stagecoach)** directly to Stoke Fleming (15 minutes)
- Ask the driver to drop you at Blackpool Sands or walk 1.5 kilometers downhill through Stoke Fleming village
- Single fare: approximately £2–3
- Timetable: https://www.stagecoachbus.com (search route 3)

**London to Blackpool Sands by public transport:**
- London Waterloo → Totnes (2 hrs 15 min, £15–40 depending on time/advance)
- Totnes → Stoke Fleming via Bus 3 (15 min, £2–3)
- Total journey: 3 hours door-to-door

### By Car

If driving from within the UK:

**From London** (160 miles) — Take the M4 toward Bath, then the A4 to Tiverton, continuing on A361/A38 toward Exeter. From Exeter, follow the above airport directions. Plan 3–3.5 hours.

**From Bristol** (80 miles) — Take the M5 south to Exeter, then follow the airport directions. Plan 1 hour 45 minutes.

**From Plymouth** (50 miles) — Take the A386 north through Ivybridge to connect with the A38/A381 toward Dartmouth. Plan 1 hour.

**Parking:** Blackpool Sands has a managed car park (not free) on the beach access road in Stoke Fleming. Standard daily rates are roughly £5–7 depending on season. Spaces are adequate year-round but fill by mid-morning on sunny summer Saturdays and bank holidays. Arrive by 10 AM during peak season to ensure parking.

### By Car from Dartmouth

If staying in Dartmouth (the nearest town with accommodations), Blackpool Sands is a 10-minute scenic drive south on the A379. The road hugs the hillside with views toward Start Point. No traffic typically, but single-track sections require patience if meeting oncoming vehicles.

## Best Time to Visit

### Seasonal Overview

**Summer (June–August)** is peak season. Daytime temperatures reach 17–20°C (63–68°F), with long daylight hours and minimal rain. Water temperature peaks at 15–16°C (59–61°F), making brief swimming feasible. Expect crowds on weekends and clear days; weekday mornings are noticeably quieter. The café operates fully, kayak rental is available, and the pines provide shade even on warm days.

**Spring (April–May)** offers mild weather (12–16°C / 54–61°F), fewer crowds, and emerging wildflowers in the cliff vegetation. Rain is possible but decreasing. Water is cold (10–12°C) but the beach is at its most peaceful. Excellent for walking, photography, and paddling if you have a wetsuit.

**Fall (September–October)** mirrors spring with comfortable temperatures (13–18°C / 55–64°F) and noticeably fewer visitors than summer. September maintains summer warmth and full facilities; October brings increasing cloud cover and occasional rain but remains excellent for coastal walks. Swell picks up—good for experienced swimmers, less ideal for timid paddlers.

**Winter (November–March)** is cool (6–10°C / 43–50°F) and wet, with frequent rain and shorter daylight. The café operates on weekends only. However, this is when the beach reveals its true character: moody skies, dramatic light, and complete solitude. The pines stand dark against grey water, and the SW Coast Path becomes a genuine adventure. Winter also brings the best storm watching if Atlantic swells reach the site.

## Where to Stay

Blackpool Sands itself has no on-beach accommodation. Instead, stay in one of three nearby towns and day-trip or stay multiple nights to explore the region.

### Dartmouth (5 kilometers, 10 minutes drive)

Dartmouth is the obvious choice: a medieval riverside town with genuine character, excellent restaurants, galleries, and the Dartmouth Steam Railway. Most of the region's quality accommodation clusters here.

**Recommended:**
- **The Dart Marina Hotel** (4-star riverside, £120–180/night) — directly on the Dart estuary with views and a restaurant
- **Royal Castle Hotel** (historic coaching inn, £80–140/night) — 16th-century hotel overlooking the harbor, character-heavy
- **Boutique guesthouses** (£70–120/night) — scattered through town, variable but mostly excellent
- **Vacation rentals** (£90–150/night) — numerous properties along the riverfront and in town center

Dartmouth is 2 kilometers from Blackpool Sands via the scenic A379. Recommend renting a car if staying overnight; limited public parking in town, easier to drive to the beach.

### Stoke Fleming (1 kilometer directly uphill)

Stoke Fleming is the village directly above Blackpool Sands—a working village with a pub (The Church House Inn, good for evening meals) and a handful of vacation rentals. Staying here puts you closest to the beach but offers fewer facilities than Dartmouth.

**Recommended:**
- **Stoke Fleming vacation rentals** — typically £85–130/night via Airbnb/Vrbo; book 6–8 weeks ahead for summer

### Kingsbridge (15 kilometers, 20 minutes drive)

Kingsbridge is a larger market town inland, less picturesque than Dartmouth but with more conventional hotels and lower prices. Better for those prioritizing budget over character.

**Recommended:**
- **Various chain hotels** (Travelodge, Premier Inn) — £60–100/night
- **The Fortescue Hotel** (independent, £70–110/night) — historic market-town hotel with charm

### Accommodation Summary

Book 6–8 weeks ahead for summer peak season (July–August). Spring and fall offer good availability at lower rates. Winter is easiest but coldest.

**Budget**: Stay in Kingsbridge or budget accommodations in Dartmouth, day-trip to the beach (£50–90/night).

**Mid-range**: Quality guesthouses in Dartmouth (£90–140/night), walk or drive to nearby attractions.

**Premium**: Riverside hotels in Dartmouth (£140–200+/night), combine beach days with estuary exploration.

## Costs

### Beach Access and Facilities

**Parking**: £5–7 per day (managed car park, Stoke Fleming)

**Café**: Light meals £8–16 (coffee, sandwiches, local ice cream), seasonal opening (full summer, weekends off-season)

**Kayak rental**: Approximately £15–20/hour or £40–60 for a half-day (subject to weather and tides; book ahead in summer)

**No entrance fee** — Blackpool Sands is free to access beyond parking.

### Nearby Services

**Dartmouth dining**: Casual lunch £12–20, dinner £20–45+ (numerous riverside restaurants, pubs)

**Groceries**: Tesco, Waitrose, and local shops in Dartmouth (5 km away) for picnic supplies (£12–25 per person)

**Attractions**: Dartmouth Steam Railway (£20–35 round trip), Agatha Christie's Greenway (£11 entry, 8 km away), SW Coast Path walks (free)

## Safety and Water Conditions

### Lifeguards and Swimming

**Lifeguards are present during summer months** (typically mid-June through early September, weekends and bank holidays). Outside this period, there is no supervised beach—swim at your own risk.

### Water and Wave Conditions

Blackpool Sands is sheltered by its bay position, making it far calmer than exposed southwest beaches. However:

- **Rip currents can occur**, particularly during spring tides and after heavy Atlantic swell. Respect the bay's configuration and ask the lifeguard (summer) about current conditions
- **Water temperature** peaks at 15–16°C (59–61°F) in August; outside June–September, hypothermia risk increases rapidly
- **Shingle bottom** means no sand bar—depth increases immediately from shore, suitable for swimmers but not ideal for very young children learning to swim
- **Tidal range** is significant (8+ meters); plan swims around tide times and check tide tables (www.tide-forecast.com)

### Cold Water and Wetsuits

Outside June–August, **wetsuits are essential** for any water immersion beyond wading. Even summer visits benefit from a 2–3mm wetsuit for extended swimming or water sports.

### Currents and Dangerous Conditions

The southwest coast is subject to Atlantic swells. While Blackpool Sands is sheltered, large Atlantic swells can wrap around Start Point. Check the **Magic Seaweed** forecast (magicseaweed.com) or **BBC Weather** before visiting, particularly in fall and winter.

## Tips and Nearby Attractions

### Dartmouth Steam Railway

A scenic 7-mile round trip on a vintage steam train between Dartmouth and Paignton (2 hours total). Runs daily in summer, weekends in spring/fall. A quintessentially English experience; book tickets in advance. Combines beautifully with a Blackpool Sands beach morning.

### Agatha Christie's Greenway

The holiday home of crime writer Agatha Christie, 8 kilometers from Blackpool Sands in the Dart Valley. Beautiful riverside estate with period furnishings and gardens. Entry £11; open April–October. Plan 2–3 hours.

### SW Coast Path

Blackpool Sands sits on the **South West Coast Path**, one of England's great long-distance walks. Easy 2-hour walks to Start Point (dramatic lighthouse, 4 km north) or south toward Slapton Sands offer spectacular coastal views. The path is well-marked and requires no special equipment.

### Local Villages

**Stoke Fleming** — the village above the beach, has a good pub (The Church House Inn) with a garden; friendly locals and real ale.

**Hallsands** — an abandoned village 3 kilometers south, destroyed by storm surge in 1917; ghostly remains and a moving memorial. Walk from Blackpool Sands via the Coast Path (2 hours round trip).

### Picnic and Self-Catering

Bring food from Dartmouth's supermarkets and eat on the beach. The café can be busy in summer. Picnicking with views toward Start Point is a quintessential Devon beach experience.

### Photography

**Golden hour** (sunrise/sunset) is exceptional. The pines frame the bay, and clear water reflects light beautifully. Winter storms create dramatic skies. The A379 between Dartmouth and the beach offers several viewpoint pulls if you want shots without descending to the beach.
