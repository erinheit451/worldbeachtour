# Senggigi Beach Travel Guide

Senggigi Beach is Lombok's gateway to the Gili Islands and the island's primary resort destination. Located on the northwestern coast, this 5-kilometer arc of sand has become the logical base for travelers exploring West Lombok, the Gili archipelago, and Mount Rinjani. Whether you're using Senggigi as a jumping-off point for island hopping, recovering from a mountain trek, or simply seeking a beach escape with better infrastructure than the south coast, Senggigi delivers practical, established beach access with views across the Lombok Strait toward Bali.

## Getting There

### By Air

Most visitors fly into **Lombok International Airport (LOP)**, located 40 kilometers south of Senggigi (approximately 60–80 minutes drive depending on traffic). This is the island's primary international hub with direct flights from Jakarta, Surabaya, Denpasar (Bali), and some regional hubs. LOP is modern and efficient, though schedules are lighter than Bali's counterpart.

Several transportation options exist from LOP:

**Official shuttle (Damri)**: The state-owned shuttle operates regular runs from LOP to Senggigi. Tickets cost IDR 200,000–250,000 (USD 12–15) and take 70–90 minutes depending on traffic and passenger load. Buses depart roughly every 2–3 hours. This is the budget-conscious, no-frills option.

**Private driver or ride-share**: Using a local driver arranged through your hotel costs IDR 400,000–600,000 (USD 25–35) for a direct route; it's faster (60 minutes) and more flexible, though prices may be higher if arranged at the airport. Ride-share apps (Grab, Gojek) are available but work best if you have a local SIM card; expect prices of IDR 350,000–500,000.

**Rental car**: Car rental at the airport costs IDR 300,000–500,000 daily (USD 18–30) for basic economy. The drive is straightforward: follow the main road north from LOP through Mataram and Ampenan, then coast along the northwestern road to Senggigi. Toll roads may add IDR 20,000–50,000. This gives flexibility but requires local driving comfort.

### By Ferry and Overland

Travelers coming from Bali can take a ferry from Padang Bai or Sanur (east Bali) to Lembar Harbor (south Lombok, 50 kilometers south of Senggigi), then arrange land transport north. Ferry prices vary (IDR 100,000–200,000 / USD 6–12) and schedules are irregular; this route adds 4–6 hours total but avoids flights.

### Access to the Gili Islands

The Gili Islands (Trawangan, Meno, Air) are 15–20 kilometers northwest of Senggigi. Daily boat transfers depart from **Bangsal Harbor**, 20 kilometers north of Senggigi (45–60 minutes by car or shuttle). Multiple operators run morning and afternoon crossings; fares are IDR 50,000–75,000 (USD 3–5) per person, though private boat charters cost 2–3x more. Travel times to the Gilis are 30–45 minutes depending on sea conditions and island destination.

## Best Time to Visit

### Seasonal Overview

**Dry Season (April–October)** is ideal. Rainfall is minimal, daily temperatures reach 28–32°C (82–90°F), and seas are calm, making boat access to the Gili Islands reliable. May–September offer the most consistent conditions; April and October transition months have occasional rain but remain generally pleasant.

**Wet Season (November–March)** brings higher rainfall (heaviest in December–February), cloud cover, and occasional rough seas that can disrupt Gili Island schedules. However, it's the shoulder season with fewer tourists and lower prices; January–February can offer quiet beach time for those unfazed by afternoon downpours.

## Where to Stay

Senggigi's 5-kilometer resort strip has a range of accommodation from budget backpackings to mid-range resorts. The beach itself and immediate surroundings are entirely developed for tourism.

### Senggigi North (Batu Layar to Bale Maro)

The northern end near Batu Layar lighthouse has quieter, more upscale resorts and private villas. This area is removed from the main beach hubbub and offers better snorkeling and diving access. A 10–20 minute walk or 5-minute drive from central Senggigi.

### Senggigi Center (Main Strip)

The heart of Senggigi hosts the majority of restaurants, dive shops, money changers, and mid-range hotels. This is where boat transfers to the Gilis are booked, tours are organized, and the beach is most developed. Busier, noisier, but most convenient.

### Senggigi South (toward Lombok Strait)

Quieter than center, with a mix of smaller hotels and private rentals. Less foot traffic, more local feel, though still solidly on the resort strip. A 10–15 minute walk south to the main beach action.

### Mataram (15 km south)

Lombok's capital offers budget hotels and local guesthouses but is further from the Gilis and beach access. Useful if arriving/departing via Damri shuttle or seeking authentic town atmosphere; most tourists stay in Senggigi itself.

### Accommodation Types

- **Budget hostels/guesthouses**: IDR 150,000–300,000/night (USD 9–18); basic rooms, often with fan only, shared bathrooms
- **Mid-range hotels**: IDR 400,000–1,000,000/night (USD 25–60); air-con, private bath, sometimes pool or small restaurant
- **Resorts and villas**: IDR 1,200,000–2,500,000+/night (USD 75–150+); full amenities, beachfront or garden, restaurant and bar
- **Vacation rentals**: IDR 500,000–1,500,000/night (USD 30–90); variable quality, often have kitchenettes

Book 2–4 weeks ahead during May–August peak season; other months offer abundant availability. Walk-in rates during low season can yield 15–30% discounts.

## Costs

### General Price Level

Senggigi is **affordable by Southeast Asian standards**. Indonesia's cost of living is low, and Senggigi—while more developed than remote beaches—doesn't command premium pricing.

### Daily Budget Breakdown

**Accommodation**: Budget USD 15–30/night for basic rooms; mid-range USD 40–80/night for air-con and amenities; upscale USD 100+/night.

**Food and beverage**: Street food and warungs (local eateries) cost IDR 30,000–60,000 (USD 2–4) per meal. Mid-range restaurants on the beach cost IDR 80,000–200,000 (USD 5–12). Western cafes and upscale dining run IDR 200,000–400,000 (USD 12–25). A picnic from a market costs IDR 50,000–80,000.

**Activities**: Gili Island boat transfer IDR 50,000–75,000 (USD 3–5). Dive certification (3–4 days) costs USD 350–500. Snorkeling tours USD 25–40. Mount Rinjani guided trek from Senaru USD 80–150 for 2–3 days all-inclusive.

**Transportation**: Local shuttle or Grab ride IDR 30,000–100,000 (USD 2–6). Car rental USD 18–30/day. Damri bus to airport IDR 200,000–250,000.

**Parking and entry**: Beach access is free. Most activities are public and unmetered.

**Overall daily budget**: USD 40–60 per person for budget travelers (food + accommodation + minimal activities); USD 80–120 for mid-range comfort.

## Safety

### Water and Swimming

Senggigi's water is generally safe for swimming in daylight hours:

- **Water temperature** ranges from 27°C (81°F) in cooler months to 30°C (86°F) in summer. This is warm year-round; wetsuits are not necessary, though rash guards offer UV protection.
- **Currents and tides** are generally mild; the Lombok Strait is sheltered compared to Bali's south coast. Always check local conditions before swimming.
- **Visibility** is good for snorkeling, though occasional plankton blooms reduce clarity.
- **Marine hazards**: Sea urchins, stonefish, and sharp coral exist; wear reef shoes. No notable shark incidents recorded.
- **Lifeguards**: Not present. Swim at your own risk, ideally with the buddy system.

Avoid swimming after dark.

### Earthquake and Tsunami Awareness

The 2018 earthquake (7.0 magnitude) caused significant damage to Senggigi's infrastructure. While recovery is complete, the region remains seismically active. The western coast is exposed to the Indian Ocean; tsunami risk is low but non-zero. Familiarize yourself with evacuation routes (typically uphill, away from shore) and follow local guidance in case of seismic activity. Odds of experiencing an earthquake are low, but the region's geology warrants baseline awareness.

### General Safety

Senggigi is considered safe for tourists. Petty theft and bag snatching occur in crowded areas; don't leave valuables unattended on the beach or in vehicles. Violent crime is rare. Normal urban caution (avoid isolated areas at night, don't flash large amounts of cash) applies.

### Health Considerations

- **Water quality**: Drink bottled water; tap water is not reliably safe.
- **Sun exposure**: UV is intense even on overcast days. Sunscreen is essential; reapply frequently.
- **Food safety**: Street food is generally safe if prepared fresh in front of you. Avoid dishes sitting at room temperature. Stomach issues affect some travelers; bring probiotics or antidiarrheals if prone to sensitivity.
- **Medical facilities**: Senggigi and nearby Mataram have basic clinics and pharmacies. Serious injuries or illnesses may require evacuation to Denpasar (Bali) or beyond.

## Local Tips

**Book Gili Island boats the evening before.** Multiple operators compete on price and schedule; walk the beach-front and compare. Morning departures (typically 8–10 AM) are most reliable.

**Mount Rinjani day hikes are not really feasible.** If interested in Rinjani, commit to a 2–3 day guided trek departing from Senaru village (40 km north). Many operators offer pre-arranged packages from Senggigi; book through your hotel.

**The southern coast (Kuta Lombok) is worth a day trip.** Pink sand, fewer tourists, excellent snorkeling. About 60 km south; rent a scooter or car, or book a tour (USD 30–50).

**Diving and snorkeling are excellent here.** Multiple dive operators offer reef dives and certification courses. Gili Air has the best house reef; Gili Meno is quieter; Gili Trawangan has nightlife.

**The northern road to Bangsal (Gili harbor) is scenic.** If you rent transport, consider driving north rather than catching a shuttle; the coastal road is beautiful and you'll spot locals fishing and working.

**Markets and local life**: Mataram's central market (Cakilang Market) is 15 km south if you want immersion in local commerce and culture. Senggigi itself is tourist-oriented; venture inland for authentic Lombok.

**Scooter rental is affordable but risky.** IDR 50,000–80,000/day (USD 3–5); helmets are legally required but inconsistently enforced. Road conditions and traffic patterns differ from Western norms; only rent if comfortable with Indonesian driving culture.

**Sunset is overrated here.** Unlike some Indonesian beaches, Senggigi's western exposure doesn't produce dramatic sunsets (Mount Agung on Bali catches the light more spectacularly). Better for sunrise or all-day beach time.

**Stay 2–4 nights minimum if using Senggigi as a Gili base.** This allows a full day on the islands without feeling rushed. The Gilis are small; one overnight is sufficient; two nights is more comfortable.
