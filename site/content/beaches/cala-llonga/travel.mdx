# Cala Llonga Travel Guide

Cala Llonga is one of Ibiza's most sheltered beaches, tucked into a deep bay on the eastern coast where pine-clad cliffs rise from turquoise Mediterranean water. Located just 5 kilometers south of Santa Eulària des Riu, this 300-meter crescent of sand offers a genuine alternative to Ibiza's more developed resort beaches—a place where families swim rather than party, where shade is natural, and where the landscape itself becomes the attraction.

## Getting There

### By Air

Most international visitors fly into **Ibiza Airport (IBZ)**, located 15.5 kilometers south of Cala Llonga. This is the island's primary gateway, with daily connections from mainland Spain (Madrid, Barcelona) and across Europe. The flight from Barcelona takes roughly 1 hour; from Madrid, 2 hours.

From the airport, several transport options serve the beach:

### By Rental Car

Rental car is the most flexible option. From Ibiza Airport:
- Follow signs toward Santa Eulària des Riu (approximately 20 kilometers, 25–35 minutes depending on traffic)
- Turn toward Cala Llonga; the road is well-signposted and becomes increasingly scenic as you approach the bay
- Parking at Cala Llonga is free and generally available, with a small lot near the beach access point

The drive from Ibiza Town (the island's capital) is roughly 30 minutes; from San Antonio, approximately 50 minutes via the PM-810 highway.

### By Bus

**Local bus service** connects Santa Eulària des Riu to Cala Llonga and nearby coastal areas. From Ibiza Airport, catch the airport bus to Santa Eulària Town Centre (approximately 20 kilometers, 30–40 minutes). From there, local connections serve Cala Llonga. Bus fares are inexpensive (€2–4 per journey) but frequencies are limited outside summer; service is more reliable June through September.

From Ibiza Town, the L23 bus runs toward the northern coast and connects to services reaching Cala Llonga area.

### By Water Taxi (Summer)

During peak season (June–September), summer boat taxis operate from Ibiza Town and other coastal destinations. These are scenic but significantly more expensive than land transport (€30–60 per person depending on distance). Booking ahead through local accommodation or tourism offices is essential.

### Driving Routes

**From Barcelona**: Take flights to Ibiza or drive to Barcelona Airport and fly. Driving via ferry is impractical (8+ hours).

**From Valencia**: Ferry to Ibiza takes approximately 5 hours; then rent a car for the 30-minute drive to Cala Llonga.

**From Madrid**: Flight to Ibiza (2 hours) is the practical option.

## Best Time to Visit

### Seasonal Overview

**Summer (June–August)** is peak season. Daytime temperatures reach 28–32°C (82–90°F), with long daylight and minimal rainfall. Water temperatures are warmest (24–26°C / 75–79°F), making extended swimming genuinely comfortable. Crowds are highest, particularly mid-July through August, but Cala Llonga remains quieter than major resort beaches.

**Late Spring (May)** and **Early Fall (September–October)** offer ideal conditions: temperatures 22–27°C (72–81°F), warm water (21–23°C / 70–73°F), and dramatically fewer visitors. September retains summer warmth while offering the relaxed atmosphere of shoulder season. October brings occasional rain but remains swimmable.

**Spring (April)** and **Late Fall (November)** see temperatures drop to 18–22°C (64–72°F). Water is noticeably cooler (17–20°C / 63–68°F). These months are less crowded and suitable for those tolerant of cooler swimming.

**Winter (December–February)** is cool and wet, with daily highs around 15°C (59°F) and frequent rain. Swimming becomes uncomfortable without a wetsuit. However, the bay's shelter means conditions remain calmer than exposed beaches; winter visitors find the landscape starkly beautiful and solitude guaranteed.

## Where to Stay

Cala Llonga itself has no hotels directly on the beach, but accommodation options exist within 2–5 kilometers in nearby towns and settlements.

### Cala Llonga Village

A handful of vacation rentals and small guest houses operate directly in Cala Llonga village, 500 meters from the beach. This is the quietest option—you're genuinely removed from tourist infrastructure but very close to the beach. Expect simple, characterful accommodation without resort amenities. €60–130/night.

### Santa Eulària des Riu

The nearest substantial town, 5 kilometers north, offers the best balance: more restaurants, shops, supermarkets, and varied accommodation (hotels, hostels, vacation rentals, guest houses). The town itself is charming and worth exploring—it's the island's hippie heritage heartland, with a Saturday market and genuinely good local restaurants. Santa Eulària feels liveable rather than touristy. €80–180/night for mid-range hotels.

### Es Canar

Just north of Santa Eulària, Es Canar is a small beach village with its own character. It has decent accommodation and direct beach access, but fewer services than Santa Eulària proper. €70–160/night.

### Ibiza Town

The island's capital, 30 kilometers southwest, offers the full range of accommodations and nightlife. Many visitors stay here and day-trip to Cala Llonga. This adds 30–40 minutes of travel time but provides vastly more evening options. €100–250+/night depending on category.

### San Antonio

On the west coast, San Antonio is party-focused and less suitable if you're seeking the quieter Cala Llonga experience. However, it's another 50 kilometers away, making it an impractical base for this beach.

### Accommodation Types

- **Vacation rentals (Airbnb, VRBO)**: €60–150/night; kitchens available for self-catering
- **Guest houses**: €70–130/night; simple, often family-run
- **Small hotels**: €80–160/night; modest amenities, character-focused
- **Hostels**: €25–50/night; budget travelers, social atmosphere in Santa Eulària

Book 4–6 weeks ahead for summer travel; shoulder seasons offer far more flexibility.

## Costs

### General Price Level

**Beach access** is free. There is no entrance fee, parking fee, or day-use charge.

### Nearby Services

**Food and beverage**: Beach bars at Cala Llonga serve drinks and light meals (€8–15 per item). Restaurants in Santa Eulària range from casual (€10–18 for mains) to upscale (€20–40+). Grocery stores in Santa Eulària allow budget picnic assembly (€12–25 per person).

**Rentals**: Paddleboard rentals from local outfitters operate seasonally; expect €20–40/hour or €50–80/day.

**Parking**: Free at the beach.

**Transportation**: Bus fares are €2–4 per journey. Rental cars start at €30–50/day for economy models.

**Overall daily budget**: €40–70 per person for a day visit (food + transit), or €100–200+ per person if staying overnight with restaurant dining.

## Safety

### Swimming Conditions

Cala Llonga's sheltered bay offers exceptionally calm swimming conditions:

- **Water temperature**: 21–26°C depending on season. Summer is genuinely warm; shoulder seasons require mild acclimation.
- **Currents and undertow**: Minimal due to the sheltered bay geography. This is one of Ibiza's safest beaches for swimmers of all abilities.
- **No significant swell**: The bay's depth and orientation mean Atlantic swell is absorbed before reaching shore.
- **Shark incidents**: Zero recorded.
- **Visibility**: Excellent; Mediterranean clarity typically allows 6–10 meters of underwater sight.
- **Lifeguards**: Not present. Swim responsibly, especially with children—use the buddy system.

### General Safety

The area is safe and well-travelled in summer. Petty theft from vehicles is rare but possible; don't leave valuables unattended. The walk to/from the beach is safe in daylight; avoid late-night solitary walks along the clifftop paths.

### Health Considerations

Water quality is monitored under EU standards and rates "good." Post-rainfall, brief closures are possible; check local updates if arriving after heavy rain.

Sunscreen is essential—the Mediterranean sun is intense even on cool days, and UV reflection off water and sand compounds exposure.

## Local Tips

**Visit in May or September.** The shoulder seasons offer warm water (21–24°C), pleasant air temperatures, and a fraction of July-August crowds. You'll genuinely enjoy the beach rather than managing it.

**The cliff walk south toward Sol d'en Serra is dramatic.** A path runs along the cliff tops south of the main bay, offering hiking and views. The terrain is easy to moderate; bring water.

**Bring shade supplies if planning extended stays.** While pines provide some natural shade on the flanks, the central beach is exposed. A parasol or beach tent is worthwhile.

**Arrive early in summer.** If visiting June–August weekends, be at the parking area by 9–10 AM; afternoon brings tour buses and crowds from larger resorts.

**The water is Mediterranean clear.** Snorkeling reveals fish, sea grass, and rocky underwater features. Bring a snorkel for extended water time.

**Santa Eulària's Saturday market is worth the short drive.** Local produce, crafts, and street food make a morning excursion worthwhile.

**Late September is often ideal.** The water is still warm, crowds have departed, and the weather remains reliable.

**Evening light is magical.** The cliffs create natural amphitheatre acoustics and the western sky lights beautifully in late evening—bring a camera.

**Local supermarkets stock supplies cheaply.** Buying grocery items in Santa Eulària is dramatically cheaper than beach vendors; picnics are the sensible budget option.
