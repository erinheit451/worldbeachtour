# Centennial Beach Travel Guide

Centennial Beach is Naperville's signature public swim destination, a historic quarry conversion that has anchored community recreation since 1931. Situated along the DuPage River at the edge of downtown Naperville, this managed beach combines family-friendly swimming with direct access to the Naperville Riverwalk—one of the Midwest's premier urban waterfront experiences. Whether you're a Chicago-area local visiting for the day or planning a longer Naperville stay, Centennial Beach offers predictable, lifeguard-supervised swimming in a setting that balances comfort and authenticity.

## Getting There

### By Air

**O'Hare International Airport (ORD)** is Chicago's primary international hub, located approximately 40 kilometers (25 miles) northeast of Centennial Beach. From ORD, expect a 50–65 minute drive via I-90 West and I-88 West during normal traffic conditions, though Chicago expressway congestion can easily extend this to 90+ minutes during rush hours (6–10 AM and 3–7 PM weekdays).

**Midway International Airport (MDW)**, located on Chicago's southwest side, is approximately 50 kilometers (31 miles) from Centennial Beach. From MDW, the drive via I-55 North to I-88 East typically takes 60–75 minutes, making it slightly more distant than ORD despite being a closer airport to central Chicago.

For those willing to rent a car from either airport, I-88 (the Reagan Memorial Tollway) is the most direct route to Naperville. Note that I-88 is a toll road; plan for $3.75–5.00 in tolls from ORD to Naperville.

### By Car

If driving from within Illinois or the broader region:

- **From Chicago downtown (Loop)**: I-290 West to I-88 West; approximately 35–50 minutes depending on traffic. I-88 is a toll road.
- **From Milwaukee, Wisconsin** (90 miles): I-94 South to I-90 East (toward Chicago), then I-88 East; approximately 2 hours.
- **From St. Louis, Missouri** (300 miles): I-55 North to I-88 East; approximately 4.5–5 hours.
- **From Indianapolis, Indiana** (185 miles): I-74 North to I-88 East; approximately 3 hours.

**Street parking** is available throughout downtown Naperville and near Centennial Beach along Richmond Avenue. Lot parking is available at Centennial Park, adjacent to the beach.

### By Public Transit

**Metra BNSF Line** provides rail access to downtown Naperville from Chicago's Union Station. The ride takes approximately 50 minutes and costs $5–7 depending on departure point. From the Naperville Metra station (Aurora Avenue), the beach is roughly 1 mile south—a 15–20 minute walk or a short local taxi ride.

**PACE buses** connect Naperville to surrounding suburbs and downtown Chicago, though service frequency is lower than Metra rail. Allow 1.5–2+ hours from downtown Chicago via PACE.

## Best Time to Visit

### Seasonal Overview

**Summer (Memorial Day weekend through Labor Day)** is the official operating season. Daytime temperatures reach 75–85°F (24–29°C), with low precipitation and long daylight hours. Water temperatures peak in August around 70–72°F (21–22°C), making swimming genuinely comfortable. Peak crowds occur on weekends and during school vacation weeks. Lifeguard service operates daily throughout this window.

**Late May and early June** offer mild temperatures (65–75°F / 18–24°C), lower crowds than peak summer, and adequate swimming conditions. Water temperatures approach 65°F (18°C), still cool but manageable for longer swims.

**Late August and early September** extend the season as Labor Day approaches. Temperatures remain warm (75–80°F / 24–27°C), water is still swimmable, and crowds begin declining after Labor Day as schools reopen.

**Spring (April–May)** and **fall (September–October)** are when the Naperville Riverwalk and adjacent Centennial Park are most pleasant for walking and cycling, though the beach itself is not open for swimming. These seasons are ideal for experiencing the broader Naperville waterfront without swim crowds.

**Winter (November–March)** features the beach park closed to swimming, though the park grounds remain accessible for walking and the adjacent Riverwalk is open year-round.

## Where to Stay

### Naperville

The city of Naperville itself offers the most convenient accommodation for beach visits. Downtown Naperville, immediately adjacent to Centennial Beach and the Riverwalk, has expanded its lodging options significantly in recent years. Mid-range hotels ($120–180/night) and vacation rentals ($130–220/night for 1–2 bedroom units) cluster near Aurora Avenue and downtown. This puts you within walking distance of both the beach and the Riverwalk's restaurants and shops.

Naperville's residential neighborhoods extend several miles in all directions, with abundant Airbnb and vacation rental availability. Staying anywhere in Naperville puts you 5–10 minutes from the beach by car or bike.

### Chicago (Day-Trip Base)

Many visitors stay in Chicago and day-trip to Centennial Beach. Downtown Chicago offers vastly more hotel variety (budget to luxury, $100–400+/night) and cultural attractions. The Metra BNSF rail connection makes car-free travel feasible: park your rental car in Chicago and take the train to Naperville. The 50-minute commute is scenic along the North Branch of the Chicago River.

### Oak Brook and Western Suburbs

Towns like Oak Brook, Downers Grove, and Hinsdale, located 8–15 kilometers west of Naperville, offer similar accommodation options with slightly lower rates ($100–160/night for mid-range hotels). These communities are 15–25 minutes from Centennial Beach by car.

### Accommodation Types

- **Mid-range hotels**: $120–180/night; chains like La Quinta, Holiday Inn, Red Roof serve the area
- **Vacation rentals/Airbnb**: $130–220/night for 1–2 bedrooms; abundant in downtown Naperville
- **Bed & breakfasts**: $110–170/night; several operate in historic Naperville homes
- **Camping**: No campgrounds immediately adjacent to Centennial Beach, but Illinois state parks and private RV facilities operate 20–40 kilometers away

Book accommodations 2–3 weeks ahead for summer weekend visits; weekday visits offer greater availability.

## Costs

### General Price Level

**Swimming access** varies by residency:

- **Naperville residents**: Generally free or minimal charge (typically under $5/day if any fee applies)
- **Non-residents**: Day-pass typically $7–12 for individuals, $12–18 for families, depending on the current Park District fee schedule
- **Season passes**: Non-resident season passes cost approximately $50–75, making them worthwhile for multiple visits

Parking is free in the adjacent Centennial Park lot and on surrounding streets.

### Nearby Services

**Food and beverage**: The Naperville Riverwalk features numerous restaurants and cafes ranging from casual ($9–18 for lunch) to casual-upscale ($20–35+ per entree). Picnic supplies from local grocery stores cost $15–30 per person. The beach itself has limited food service; planning a picnic is typical for day visitors.

**Equipment rentals**: Bicycle rentals ($15–25/day) are available throughout downtown Naperville, making it easy to explore the broader Riverwalk system. The Naperville Park District occasionally offers paddleboard and kayak rentals on the DuPage River in nearby locations, though not directly at Centennial Beach.

**Lodging**: See "Where to Stay" section above for detailed pricing.

## Safety & Swimming Conditions

Centennial Beach maintains year-round water quality testing and lifeguard supervision throughout the official swim season (Memorial Day–Labor Day). The Naperville Park District enforces a **deep-end swim test**: swimmers attempting to access the 15-foot drop-off must demonstrate basic swimming proficiency to the lifeguards, ensuring that only strong swimmers venture into the deeper section.

This policy—uncommon at many public beaches—reflects the site's quarry history and the genuine depth variance. Children should wear Coast Guard-approved flotation devices in all areas until they've completed the deep-end test.

**Water temperature**: In early summer (May–early June), water remains cool (60–65°F). A rash guard or light wetsuit extends comfort significantly. By July and August, water temperatures reach 70–72°F, warm enough for extended swimming without thermal protection.

**Currents and conditions**: The quarry's enclosed, dammed basin means no ocean currents or tidal hazards. Water conditions are typically stable throughout the day.

## Tips & Nearby Attractions

### The Naperville Riverwalk

Centennial Beach's greatest asset is its integration into the **Naperville Riverwalk**, a 1.9-mile paved trail that runs along the DuPage River from Naperville's central business district to Rotary Hill (upstream). The Riverwalk features:

- Restaurants and cafes at river level
- Bicycle and pedestrian paths (completely separated from car traffic)
- Scenic overlooks and photo opportunities
- Evening illumination (seasonal)
- River access for paddling and fishing

Combining a beach visit with a Riverwalk walk, dinner, or bike ride creates a complete day in Naperville.

### Naper Settlement

Just northwest of Centennial Beach (about 2 kilometers), **Naper Settlement** is a historic village museum featuring 25+ preserved buildings spanning Naperville's 1831–1900s settlement era. Admission is approximately $15–20 and provides 2–3 hours of exploration. This site contextualizes Centennial Beach within Naperville's broader history.

### Centennial Park

Immediately adjacent to the beach, **Centennial Park** features playground equipment, picnic areas, shade pavilions, and open lawn—ideal for pre-swim or post-swim family time. The park is free and open year-round.

### Cycling the Greater Naperville Network

The Naperville Park District maintains over 100 miles of connected trails throughout the community. Renting a bike and exploring trails along the East Branch of the DuPage River or toward nearby preserves makes for excellent half-day excursions. Multiple bike shops in downtown Naperville rent for $15–25/day.

### Chicago Day-Trip Option

If staying overnight in Naperville or making this a multi-day trip, downtown Chicago is 35–50 minutes away by car. The museums, Navy Pier, Millennium Park, and Lake Michigan shores provide obvious complementary attractions.
