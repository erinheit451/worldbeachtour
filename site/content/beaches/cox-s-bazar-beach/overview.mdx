Cox's Bazar Beach is one of the world's longest natural sandy beaches, stretching approximately 120 kilometers along the Bay of Bengal in southeastern Bangladesh. This extraordinary expanse of unbroken shoreline makes it a geographical marvel and a destination that defies the typical beach experience found elsewhere in South Asia.

Located just 2.8 kilometers from the town of Cox's Bazar and easily accessible via Cox's Bazar Airport (3.1 km away), the beach sits in Chittagong Division, positioned at the intersection of the Bay of Bengal's warming waters and Bangladesh's tropical climate. The beach's scale is almost impossible to grasp until you're standing on it—the horizon stretches endlessly, and even during busy periods, you can find stretches of complete solitude.

## What to Expect

The beach is characterized by its gentle slope and wide, open expanse—perfect for walking, running, and swimming. The substrate is primarily sandy with a firm, compact surface ideal for vehicle travel, which has historically made it one of the few beaches in the world where cars are regularly driven. The water is warm year-round, with the Bay of Bengal offering generally calm conditions, though the monsoon season (June to September) brings rougher seas.

The experience here is distinctly different from crowded tourist beaches elsewhere in Asia. While Cox's Bazar town buzzes with activity—hotels, restaurants, and shops catering to Bangladeshi and international visitors—the beach itself often feels remarkably underdeveloped and raw. This can be liberating for travelers seeking authenticity over infrastructure.

## Relevant Travel Lenses

**Travel & Culture**: The beach's accessibility and proximity to a functional town make it practical for travelers of varying budgets. The contrast between the vast natural landscape and the modest development tells a story about tourism's arrival in Bangladesh.

**Photography**: The sheer scale and emptiness offer remarkable compositional opportunities. Golden hour light across 120 kilometers of sand, dramatic monsoon skies, and the interplay between human activity and natural vastness create compelling visual narratives.

**Family**: The gentle slopes, warm water, and generally safe conditions make this suitable for families, though amenities vary significantly along the beach's length.

**Sand & Landscape**: This is fundamentally a beach about scale and geological presence. The sand itself, the way it holds the imprint of the sea, and the relentless horizon are the main attractions here.

**Environment**: As one of the world's longest beaches, understanding its role in the Bay of Bengal ecosystem, its vulnerability to climate change, and its importance to local fishing communities provides important context for visitors.
