Playa de las Teresitas is one of Tenerife's most distinctive beaches, and for good reason. Located just 8 kilometers north of Santa Cruz de Tenerife in Spain's Canary Islands, this golden-sand crescent sits in a sheltered bay backed by dramatic volcanic cliffs. What sets it apart from typical Atlantic beaches is its unusual origin: the sand was imported from the Sahara Desert in the 1970s to create an ideal bathing beach where none naturally existed. The result is a remarkable 1.5-kilometer stretch of fine golden sand that feels almost tropical, despite Tenerife's volcanic landscape.

The beach serves as a natural focal point between the urban energy of Santa Cruz and the rugged beauty of the surrounding Anaga region. The proximity to the city—just a short bus ride or scenic drive—makes it accessible for day trips, yet the beach maintains a more relaxed, local atmosphere than some of Tenerife's busier tourist beaches. The calm, shallow waters of the bay are protected by natural breakwaters, making them considerably gentler than the exposed Atlantic swells found elsewhere on the island.

## What to Expect

This is fundamentally a **beach for relaxation and swimming**. The sandy bottom, clear waters, and lifeguard presence create an ideal family environment. You'll find essential amenities including showers and restrooms, plus nearby restaurants and chiringuitos (beach bars) serving fresh seafood and local specialties. The beach attracts a mix of locals, families, and travelers seeking an authentic Canary Islands experience without the crowded resort atmosphere.

The surrounding area reveals why this beach captures attention: the dramatic cliffs of the Anaga mountains rise behind you, creating one of the most photogenic coastal backdrops in the region. Bird enthusiasts will spot Lesser Black-backed Gulls and other seabirds, while the marine ecosystem supports around 20 documented species.

## Relevant Lenses

**Family Travel** stands out as the primary lens—shallow protected waters, facilities, and lifeguard coverage make this ideal for children.

**Photography** is secondary but compelling; the contrast between golden sand, azure water, and volcanic cliffs offers excellent compositions, particularly during golden hour.

**Environment and Nature** matter for understanding this beach's unique character. The imported Saharan sand is itself a fascinating ecological story, and the Anaga cliffs represent one of Tenerife's most biodiverse regions.

**Travel** broadly applies for anyone seeking an accessible yet authentic Canary Islands beach experience without traveling to remote locations.
