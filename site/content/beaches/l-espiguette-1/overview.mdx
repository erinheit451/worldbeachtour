Plage de l'Espiguette is a 10-kilometer wild dune beach stretching along the Mediterranean coast near Le Grau-du-Roi in the Camargue, a UNESCO-recognized natural region in southern France. This is not a developed resort beach—it's a raw, windswept expanse of sand and dunes sheltered within the Camargue Natural Regional Park, where the landscape remains largely untouched and the beach sustains its ecological integrity.

The beach is named after L'Espiguette, a historic lighthouse built in 1868 that stands as a navigational landmark and symbol of the Camargue's maritime heritage. The lighthouse anchors the northern end of this wild coastline, where European traditions of clothing-optional bathing have established themselves alongside family areas, creating a uniquely continental atmosphere.

What makes Plage de l'Espiguette distinctive is its wildness preserved within a natural protection framework. The dune ecosystem supports endemic plant life, particularly *Echinophora spinosa* and *Euphorbia paralias*, species adapted to salt spray and sandy substrate. The shallow Mediterranean waters support 20 observed species, ranging from invertebrates like sepia and sand gobies to migratory seabirds, making this beach ecologically significant as well as recreational.

Just 4 kilometers away, **Port Camargue**—Europe's largest privately-owned marina—stands in stark contrast. With thousands of moorings for sailboats and yachts, it represents the Mediterranean's active maritime economy while Plage de l'Espiguette remains deliberately undeveloped. This juxtaposition is central to the beach's character: accessibility to services without infrastructure encroachment.

**Most Relevant Lenses:**

**Travel & Recreation** — Plage de l'Espiguette caters to visitors seeking authentic Mediterranean beach culture without commercialization. Accessibility to Le Grau-du-Roi's services combined with the beach's wildness makes it ideal for both day-trippers and extended stays.

**Naturism** — This beach has established clothing-optional sections that are widely respected and well-integrated into the broader beach community. European naturism conventions are honored here without segregation; visitors of all preferences coexist peacefully.

**Environment** — The Camargue Natural Regional Park designation and the beach's protection within park boundaries mean the dune ecosystem and species populations are actively managed for conservation. This beach is part of a larger landscape—visit the nearby Parc Ornithologique to see pink flamingos and other species thriving inland.

**Solitude & Wildness** — For those seeking escape from crowded beaches, the 10-kilometer stretch offers genuine solitude, particularly in shoulder seasons. Wind-sculpted dunes and the lighthouse at the far end create a landscape more reminiscent of wilderness than resort.

Plage de l'Espiguette succeeds because it has resisted commercialization while remaining accessible. It is a beach for swimmers, naturists, photographers, and ecologically-minded travelers—a place where the Mediterranean feels genuinely wild.
