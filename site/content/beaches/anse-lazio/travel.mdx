# Anse Lazio Travel Guide

Anse Lazio ranks among the world's most celebrated beaches, regularly appearing in lists of the planet's finest destinations. Located on Praslin Island in Seychelles, this crescent of white sand framed by massive granite boulders and takamaka trees delivers the complete tropical beach experience. Whether you're drawn by its consistent appearance in global rankings, the dramatic geology, or simply the reputation for pristine waters and soft sand, Anse Lazio lives up to its legendary status. The beach sits at the intersection of accessibility and authenticity—close enough to arrive directly from international flights, yet maintaining the character of a natural beach rather than a developed resort strip.

## Getting There

### International Routes

Most visitors arrive via **Seychelles International Airport (SEZ)** on Mahé Island, approximately 45 kilometers southwest of Anse Lazio (roughly 90 minutes of travel time including ferry or domestic flight). SEZ is the primary international gateway, served by Air Seychelles, Qatar Airways, Emirates, and other carriers with connections through Middle Eastern and European hubs.

From SEZ, you have two primary onward routes:

**Option 1: Ferry then taxi (most economical)**
- **Cat Cocos ferry service** operates daily between Mahé and Praslin, departing from the Victoria waterfront port. Journey time is approximately 1 hour; ferries typically depart mid-morning and afternoon. Fares run around 500–650 SCR (USD $35–45) per person.
- Upon arrival at Baie Ste Anne on Praslin's east coast, taxis are available at the ferry terminal for the 20-minute drive to Anse Lazio (approximately 800–1,000 SCR / USD $55–70).
- Total time from SEZ to beach: approximately 2–2.5 hours. Total cost: roughly USD $90–115 per person.

**Option 2: Domestic flight (fastest but pricier)**
- **Air Seychelles** operates domestic flights between Mahé (SEZ airport) and Praslin Island Airport (PRI), a journey of roughly 15 minutes. Fares range from 4,000–6,000 SCR (USD $280–420) per person depending on advance booking.
- From PRI, the airport is just 3 kilometers from Anse Lazio—a 5-minute taxi ride (roughly 150–200 SCR / USD $10–14).
- Total time from SEZ to beach: approximately 1 hour including airport procedures. Total cost: roughly USD $290–435 per person.

### Ground Transport on Praslin

Praslin has no public bus system serving Anse Lazio directly. Transportation options are:

- **Taxis**: Available at the ferry terminal (Baie Ste Anne) and Praslin Island Airport. Negotiate fares before entering; standard rates run 800–1,200 SCR ($55–85 USD) from either arrival point to Anse Lazio.
- **Rental cars**: Available through guesthouses and small rental agencies on Praslin. Expect 300–400 SCR/day (USD $20–28). Roads are well-maintained but narrow; driving is on the left.
- **Hotel shuttles**: Some accommodations in nearby towns (Anse Volbert, Côte d'Or) offer shuttle services; confirm availability when booking.

### Getting to the Beach Itself

Anse Lazio has no parking lot directly on the sand. Free parking exists along the coastal road above the beach—typically 2–3 spots available during low-season, potentially full during peak summer. Walk down marked paths to the sand (approximately 50–100 meters depending on tide level).

## Best Time to Visit

### Seasonal Overview

**April–May (Dry Season)**
Light winds, calm seas, minimal rainfall, and pleasant temperatures (28–30°C / 82–86°F) make April–May arguably the most reliable season. This shoulder season offers the best combination of weather and manageable crowds. Humidity is moderate, and water temperatures are warm (26–27°C / 79–81°F).

**October–November (Secondary Dry Season)**
The transition between the southwest and northeast monsoons creates another favorable window. Temperatures are similar (27–29°C / 81–84°F), and rainfall is low. This period is slightly less crowded than April–May but equally reliable for weather.

**December–March (Northeast Monsoon / Peak Season)**
Warm temperatures (29–31°C / 84–88°F) and higher rainfall characterize this period. December and January attract significant tourist traffic. February–March sees declining crowds as rainfall increases and some European visitors depart post-holidays. Water is warmest (27–28°C / 81–82°C) but weather is less predictable.

**June–September (Southwest Monsoon)**
Stronger winds, occasional heavy rain, and rougher seas dominate. Temperatures are slightly cooler (26–28°C / 79–82°F). This is the least visited period; the beach is virtually empty but conditions are less pleasant. Swells increase, making the water rougher, though diving conditions improve due to enhanced visibility.

## Where to Stay

Anse Lazio itself has no resort or accommodation directly on the beach. However, several towns within 10–20 kilometers offer diverse options:

### Anse Volbert (Nord Est, 8 km away)
The closest significant tourist infrastructure, Anse Volbert features a sandy beach, restaurants, and small hotels. Most guesthouses and vacation rentals in Praslin cluster here. Anse Volbert is 10–15 minutes by car from Anse Lazio. Accommodation ranges from budget guesthouses (USD $60–100/night) to mid-range hotels (USD $120–200/night).

### Côte d'Or (Baie Ste Anne area, 12 km away)
The main residential and commercial center of Praslin, Côte d'Or sits on the sheltered eastern coast. It has more restaurants and shops than Anse Volbert, though is slightly further from Anse Lazio (15–20 minutes by car). Guesthouses here typically charge USD $70–150/night.

### Baie Ste Anne (Eastern coast, 15 km away)
The ferry terminal hub, Baie Ste Anne is functional rather than scenic but has basic accommodation and restaurants. It's further from Anse Lazio but serves as a logistics hub if you're island-hopping.

### Accommodation Types and Price Range

- **Guesthouses/small hotels**: USD $60–150/night; locally owned, basic but clean, often include breakfast
- **Mid-range hotels**: USD $120–250/night; private bungalows, restaurant, basic amenities
- **Vacation rentals**: USD $80–200/night; rented directly by owners or through booking platforms; variable quality
- **Luxury resorts**: USD $300–800+/night; available on Praslin but not beachfront at Anse Lazio proper; concentrate in other areas

Book accommodations at least 4 weeks in advance for December–January and April–May. June–September offers far more availability. Many smaller guesthouses fill via direct email rather than online platforms.

## Costs

### General Price Level

Seychelles is one of the world's most expensive island destinations. The combination of island geography, limited local production, and high-end tourism infrastructure drives prices significantly above African or Southeast Asian comparables.

**Daily budget breakdown (per person):**
- **Budget traveler**: USD $120–180 (guesthouse, local food, limited activities)
- **Mid-range**: USD $200–350 (modest hotel, restaurant meals, rental car, activities)
- **Luxury**: USD $400+ (upscale accommodation, fine dining, private guides, diving)

### Specific Costs

**Accommodation**: USD $60–800/night depending on category (see "Where to Stay" section above).

**Food**: Restaurant meals range from casual lunch plates (150–250 SCR / USD $10–17) to upscale dinners (500–1,500 SCR / USD $35–100+). Grocery store items are imported and expensive; a simple picnic lunch costs USD $15–25 per person.

**Transportation**: Taxi fares between towns are 300–1,200 SCR (USD $20–85). Rental cars cost 300–500 SCR/day (USD $20–35).

**Activities**: Snorkeling is often free from the beach; guided dives cost 1,000–1,500 SCR (USD $70–100) per dive through dive operators based in other towns. Hiking to viewpoints is typically free.

**Getting to the beach**: Free parking, no entrance fee.

## Safety

### Water Conditions & Marine Hazards

**Currents and rip tides**: Anse Lazio is exposed at both northern and southern ends. Currents can develop at the rocky extremities, particularly during incoming or outgoing tides. Swim in the central beach area rather than near the boulder formations. Always observe the water before entering and swim with a companion.

**Reef**: Sharp coral reef exists at the northern and southern boundaries of the beach. Water shoes are strongly recommended for reef walks; cuts are common and risk infection in tropical climates. The reef supports fish populations but is not suitable for casual wading near the edges.

**Box jellyfish**: Rarely reported in Seychelles waters, but jellyfish do occasionally appear during certain seasons. If you observe jellyfish, exit the water and ask local guides before returning. Wetsuits provide some protection.

**Sharks**: Zero recorded shark incidents at Anse Lazio.

**Water temperature & hypothermia risk**: Not applicable; water temperatures consistently exceed 26°C (79°F) even in coolest months.

**Visibility**: Clear waters support reef viewing, though underwater visibility varies with weather and time of day. Post-storm conditions may reduce clarity temporarily.

### General Beach Safety

- Swim only during daylight hours in the central beach area
- Avoid the boulder formations at both ends unless experienced with rocky terrain
- Use reef shoes or sandals rather than bare feet
- Apply high-SPF sunscreen repeatedly; tropical UV is intense and deceptive on cooler-feeling days
- Don't leave unattended valuables on the beach; petty theft occurs (though violent crime is extremely rare)

### Health Precautions

Medical facilities on Praslin are basic; serious issues require evacuation to Mahé. Travel insurance covering evacuation is strongly recommended. The nearest clinic is in Côte d'Or (15 km away). Pharmacies in Baie Ste Anne and Côte d'Or stock basic medications.

Tap water in most guesthouses is safe, though bottled water is widely available and inexpensive (USD $0.50–1 per bottle).

## Local Tips

**Visit Vallée de Mai National Park.** This UNESCO World Heritage Site protecting the endemic Coco de Mer coconut is just south of Praslin; a 2–3 hour walk through forest reveals the botanical specialization that makes Seychelles unique. Entrance fee is approximately 400 SCR (USD $28).

**Combine with nearby Anse Georgette.** Another exceptional beach on Praslin's northern coast is Anse Georgette, a 15-minute walk north of Anse Lazio along the coastal path. It's smaller and quieter; combining both beaches in one beach day is common.

**Bring reef shoes or water sandals.** The coral and rocks at beach boundaries make bare feet risky. Shoes are more comfortable than flip-flops in reef environments.

**Rent a car if planning multiple beaches.** Taxis between locations add up financially; a 1–2 day rental car (USD $20–35/day) provides flexibility to explore Anse Volbert, Côte d'Or beaches, and driving to Vallée de Mai trailheads.

**Expect minimal nightlife.** This is not a party destination. Entertainment centers around restaurant meals and sunset watching. Bring books or activities if evening entertainment is important to you.

**Ferry to La Digue Island.** The small, car-free island of La Digue (45-minute ferry from Praslin) offers Anse Source d'Argent, another world-famous beach. Many visitors do multi-island tours combining Praslin and La Digue.

**Plan arrival carefully.** International flights to SEZ typically arrive evening; ferry schedules may not align. Consider overnight accommodation in Victoria (Mahé capital) and ferry next morning, or book the domestic flight option despite higher cost.

**Money exchange**: Bring USD or EUR and exchange at airport or banks; local currency (Seychellois Rupee, SCR) has limited ATM availability. 1 USD ≈ 14 SCR (approximate; rates fluctuate).

**Respecting locals**: Praslin is a small island with permanent communities. Be respectful of local life—don't photograph people without permission, avoid loud behavior in villages, and support local businesses.
