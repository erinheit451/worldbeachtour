# Cable Beach Travel Guide

Cable Beach is Nassau's most developed and accessible beach destination, offering a full resort experience with calm Caribbean waters, abundant facilities, and direct access to the Bahamas' tourism infrastructure. Located on the northern shore of New Providence, this 4-kilometer stretch of white sand serves as the island's primary hospitality hub, drawing resort guests, day-trippers, and water sports enthusiasts. Unlike more remote Bahamian beaches, Cable Beach prioritizes accessibility and convenience—a deliberate choice that makes it the logical entry point for visitors unfamiliar with the islands.

## Getting There

### By Air

The vast majority of Cable Beach visitors arrive via **Lynden Pindling International Airport (NAS)**, located just 7.5 kilometers south of the beach—approximately 15–20 minutes by car or taxi. NAS is the Caribbean's third-busiest airport and serves as the primary hub for flights from the North American mainland. Major carriers including American, Delta, Southwest, JetBlue, and Caribbean-focused operators (Bahamas Air, Sky Bahamas) operate regular service.

Direct flights to NAS are available from:
- **Miami (MIA)** — 50 minutes; most common entry point
- **Fort Lauderdale (FLL)** — 45 minutes
- **Atlanta (ATL)** — 2.5 hours; hub carrier
- **New York (JFK/LGA/EWR)** — 3–3.5 hours
- **Boston (BOS)** — 3.5 hours
- **Toronto (YYZ)** — 4 hours

### Ground Transportation from NAS

**Taxis**: Metered taxi service is available immediately outside the arrivals concourse. A taxi ride from NAS to Cable Beach costs approximately $20–25 USD (official rate roughly $2/km). Confirm the rate with the dispatcher before entering the vehicle. The ride takes 15–20 minutes in normal traffic.

**Car Rental**: All major rental companies (Hertz, Avis, Budget, Enterprise) operate counters in the terminal. Vehicle costs range $40–80 USD/day for economy models; gas is more expensive than the US mainland. Driving from NAS to Cable Beach is straightforward: head north on Paradise Island Drive, then west on West Bay Street. The route is well-marked and uncongested outside peak hours.

**Ride-Share**: Uber operates in Nassau with slightly higher rates than taxi but trackable pricing. Expect $18–28 USD from NAS to Cable Beach.

**Shuttle Services**: Most resort hotels offer shuttle services for guests; confirm at your booking. These are generally included or discounted for resort guests.

### Access Within Nassau

Cable Beach is accessible via West Bay Street, a well-maintained highway that runs the length of Nassau's north shore. From downtown Nassau (about 10 kilometers southeast), the drive takes 15–20 minutes. The beach is not served by public bus with frequency similar to downtown, though some local jitneys (shared minibuses) operate routes along Bay Street. Expect fares of $1–2 USD for local transit.

Driving is the most practical option for accessing Cable Beach multiple times during a stay. Gas is expensive; budget roughly $3.50–4.00 USD per gallon.

## Best Time to Visit

### Seasonal Overview

**December–April (Dry Season)** is peak tourism season throughout the Bahamas. This is when most North American and international visitors arrive. Weather is warm (75–85°F / 24–29°C), with low rainfall and comfortable humidity. Ocean temperatures range from 74–77°F (23–25°C)—genuinely swimmable without a wetsuit. Expect crowds at Cable Beach, particularly during Christmas/New Year (Dec 20–Jan 2) and spring break (March–April). Hotels are fully booked; reserve accommodations 2–3 months ahead.

**May–June (Shoulder Season)** transitions toward summer. Temperatures rise (80–88°F / 27–31°C), humidity increases slightly, and rain becomes more frequent but still manageable. Tourist numbers drop significantly; this is an ideal window for visitors who want warmth without peak crowds. Hotel rates fall 20–30% compared to peak season.

**July–August (Hot Summer)** brings high temperatures (88–92°F / 31–33°C), elevated humidity, and afternoon thunderstorms. The summer storm season creates brief but intense daily rain—usually clearing within 1–2 hours. These months see lower tourism and lower hotel rates; however, conditions are hot and less comfortable for extended outdoor time. The water is warm (82–84°F / 28–29°C).

**September–November (Hurricane Season)** is technically hurricane season, though direct hits on Nassau are rare (roughly once per decade). This period sees the lowest tourism, lowest hotel rates, and the highest rainfall. Actual hurricane impacts are uncommon, but tropical weather patterns bring scattered storms. Late October and November often feature beautiful, clear weather as storm systems track westward without affecting the Bahamas. September sees occasional rain but temperatures are warm and beaches are uncrowded.

## Where to Stay

Cable Beach is home to multiple resort properties, making it easy to base your stay directly on the beach. Additionally, nearby Paradise Island (connected by bridge) and downtown Nassau offer alternative accommodation options.

### Cable Beach Resorts

The primary resort cluster sits directly on the sand with full-service facilities, restaurants, and water sports operators.

**Meliá Nassau Beach All-Inclusive** — Multi-story all-inclusive property with beach access, multiple pools, restaurants, and activities included. Rates: $200–400/night depending on season and room category. All-inclusive model means meals and some drinks are covered.

**Atlantis Resort** — Iconic massive resort property (technically on Paradise Island, but immediately adjacent to Cable Beach across a bridge) offering luxury accommodations, extensive facilities, and premium pricing. Rates: $300–800+/night. Expensive but full-service with aquariums, water parks, and fine dining.

**SLS Baha Mar** — Luxury property with modern design, multiple restaurants, casino, and full beach access. Rates: $250–600/night. Recently renovated; appeals to upscale travelers.

Smaller properties and vacation rentals also operate along Cable Beach with lower rates ($80–200/night) but fewer amenities than major resorts.

### Paradise Island

Directly across the bridge from Cable Beach, Paradise Island hosts additional resort options and has a more developed dining/shopping district. A 5-minute drive or 15-minute walk connects Paradise Island to Cable Beach.

### Downtown Nassau

Approximately 10 kilometers southeast, downtown Nassau (around Nassau Straw Market and Bay Street) offers budget hotels ($60–150/night), dive shops, and local restaurants. It's less beach-oriented but more culturally immersive. A taxi ride to Cable Beach costs $20–25 from downtown.

## Costs

### General Price Level

The Bahamas is expensive compared to continental North America. Plan for higher costs than a similarly developed beach destination in Florida or Mexico.

### Accommodation

- **All-inclusive resorts**: $200–400/night (covers meals + drinks)
- **Upscale hotels**: $250–600/night (rooms only; meals not included)
- **Moderate hotels**: $120–250/night
- **Budget/vacation rentals**: $70–150/night
- **Resorts sometimes offer package deals**: Flight + hotel bundles can provide modest savings (5–15%)

### Food & Beverage

- **Resort dining**: $15–30 per entree; high markup
- **Casual beachside restaurants**: $12–25 per meal
- **Local restaurants (downtown Nassau)**: $10–18 per meal; better value
- **Groceries**: Expensive; a meal for two from a grocery store costs $20–35
- **Drinks at bars**: $6–12 per cocktail; resort bars run toward the high end

### Activities & Services

- **Water sports rentals** (jet ski, paddleboard, parasailing): $50–150 per activity or per hour
- **Guided snorkel tours**: $80–150 per person
- **Taxi services**: $2/km metered rate or negotiated flat rates
- **Car rental**: $40–80/day plus expensive gas

### Daily Budget Estimate

- **Budget traveler** (off-season, local restaurants): $80–120/day
- **Moderate visitor** (mid-range resort, casual dining): $180–250/day
- **Upscale visitor** (resort dining, activities): $300–500+/day

Peak season (December–April) rates are 30–50% higher than shoulder/off-season rates.

## Safety

### Water Safety

Cable Beach's calm conditions create a false sense of security. Important safety considerations:

- **Water temperature** is warm year-round (74–84°F / 23–29°C), minimizing hypothermia risk. However, sun exposure is intense; UV protection is essential.
- **No lifeguards** are officially on duty at Cable Beach, though resort properties sometimes station attendants in summer. Never assume anyone is watching.
- **Currents** are generally mild but can form near shallow areas and reef edges. Ask locals or resort staff about current conditions before swimming.
- **Shark incidents** have occurred in Bahamian waters; however, Cable Beach is a heavily trafficked commercial area where serious incidents are rare. Hundreds of swimmers use the beach daily without incident.
- **Stonefish and sea urchins** are possible in shallow water; wear water shoes.
- **Saltwater quality**: Post-heavy-rain, water quality can briefly decline. Ask resort staff if conditions are safe.

Swim during daylight hours, avoid dawn and dusk, and use the buddy system.

### Personal Safety

Cable Beach and surrounding areas are generally safe for tourists in daylight and populated areas. Standard precautions apply:

- Don't display expensive jewelry, cameras, or electronics on the beach.
- Don't leave valuables unattended.
- Avoid walking alone at night, particularly away from the main resort strip.
- Downtown Nassau has more petty crime; use taxis rather than walking after dark.
- Police presence is routine on Cable Beach during peak hours.

Resort areas are well-lit and patrolled; safety is high during business hours.

### Health Considerations

- **Sun exposure**: UV intensity in the Caribbean is high even on partly cloudy days. Apply SPF 50+ reef-safe sunscreen every 2 hours and after swimming.
- **Dehydration**: Tropical heat and sun cause rapid dehydration. Drink water constantly; many visitors underestimate fluid needs.
- **Mosquitoes**: Dengue and Zika are potential concerns. Insect repellent with DEET is recommended, particularly if visiting off the main beach strip.
- **Healthcare**: Medical facilities exist in Nassau. Travel insurance is strongly recommended to cover evacuation if needed.

## Local Tips

**Visit during the dry season (Dec–April) for the most reliable weather, but expect crowds and higher prices.** If you prefer solitude or lower costs, May–June or October–November offer good weather with fewer visitors.

**Base yourself at a resort with beach access.** This maximizes convenience and gives you a fixed point for exploring the wider island. Day-trips to other beaches or Nassau's attractions are easy to arrange.

**Book water sports and tours through your resort.** Hotel concierges can arrange activities at fair rates and with safety assurance. Independent booking is possible but requires more navigation.

**The resorts provide all necessary facilities—restrooms, showers, food, water sports.** You don't need to leave Cable Beach for a full day unless you want to explore downtown Nassau or other island areas.

**Rent a car if you plan to explore beyond Cable Beach.** Taxis are convenient but expensive for repeated trips. A rental car is cost-effective after 3–4 rides.

**Paradise Island is directly adjacent and worth a day visit.** The bridge connection makes it easy to explore the Atlantis facilities and beaches without extensive driving.

**The water is calmest in the morning.** Afternoon wind occasionally creates chop. Early swimmers have better conditions and fewer crowds.

**Bring reef-safe sunscreen.** Standard sunscreen damages coral ecosystems; reef-safe formulas protect both your skin and the marine environment.

**Cable Beach's main advantage is convenience, not wilderness.** If you're seeking isolation or undeveloped coastal experience, other Bahamian beaches (Eleuthera, Exuma, Long Island) offer that character better. Cable Beach succeeds as a developed resort destination.

**The local conch salad is iconic.** Multiple restaurants serve it beachside; it's fresh and worth trying.

**Plan activities around the weather.** Even in dry season, brief afternoon thunderstorms occur. Build flexibility into your day.
