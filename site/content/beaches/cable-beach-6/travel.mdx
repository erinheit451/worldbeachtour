# Cable Beach Travel Guide

## Getting There

Broome International Airport (BME) is the gateway—3.2 kilometers from Cable Beach with direct flights from Perth (3 hours) and Darwin (2 hours). Major Australian carriers operate scheduled routes; flights tend to be expensive but essential given the distance involved. Broome sits 2,000 kilometers northeast of Perth and 1,700 kilometers south of Darwin, making it Australia's most remote major tourist destination.

The Great Northern Highway connects Broome to Perth (2,000 km south) and Darwin (1,700 km north)—brutal long-haul drives. Most visitors fly; the minority driving should plan 48 hours minimum for either route. The highway is sealed but remote; fuel and accommodation are essential planning items.

## Best Time to Visit

**April to October (Dry Season)** is Cable Beach's sweet spot. The Kimberley dry season brings stable weather, comfortable temperatures (18–28°C), and low humidity. April-May and August-September are ideal: warm enough for camel rides but without peak summer crowds.

**Avoid November to March (Wet Season & Stinger Season).** The Kimberley wet brings cyclone risk, sudden thunderstorms, and flooding. More critically, box jellyfish inhabit northern Australian waters October-May; stinger season protocols require checking current advisories before entering the water. Saltwater crocodiles are present year-round and particularly active during breeding season (October-March).

## Where to Stay

**Cable Beach Resorts & Hotels** — Accommodation options cluster directly on the beach; Cable Beach Shacks, Cable Beach Caravan Park, and mid-range resorts offer direct sand access. These are premium-priced (AUD $150–300+ per night) but eliminate the commute and allow flexibility around tides.

**Broome Old Town & Chinatown** — 4.4 kilometers away, Broome's historic core offers heritage guesthouses, backpacker hostels, and budget options. The tradeoff: no beach proximity but access to local character, restaurants, and infrastructure.

**Cost Reality** — Broome is expensive. Remote location + limited supply means accommodation, food, and activities cost 30–50% above Australian mainland averages. Budget AUD $200–250 per day (bed + food) for comfortable travel.

## Safety Considerations

**Box Jellyfish (October–May Stinger Season)** — Coastal waters October-May host irukandji and box jellyfish. Check the Department of Health's stinger advisory and Broome's lifeguard bulletins before swimming. Most beaches post seasonal closures; respect them entirely. Protective suits (rashguards) reduce risk but don't eliminate it.

**Saltwater Crocodiles** — Northern Australian waters host saltwater crocodiles (estuarine crocs), dangerous and potentially fatal. Crocodiles are present year-round, concentrated in estuaries and mangrove areas but occasionally inhabiting open beaches. Never enter water alone; check current advisories and heed all crocodile warnings.

**Sand Driving (4WD vehicles)** — The northern sections of Cable Beach are accessible via 4WD only at low tide. Soft sand, tidal traps, and remote terrain create hazards; inexperienced drivers face getting bogged or stranded. Only attempt with local guides or established 4WD tour operators.

**Tidal Conditions** — The 10-meter tidal range creates dynamic beach conditions. Sand banks shift; low tide reveals vast beaches, but rising tide can trap unwary drivers or swimmers. Plan activities around tide times; don't assume any beach area is permanently accessible.

## Travel Tips

**Camel Rides** — Red Sun Camels and Broome Camel Safaris run daily sunset rides along the beach. These operate at low tide on scheduled sunset times; book ahead during peak season (May-September). Costs are AUD $70–120 per person. The experience is genuinely iconic and should be the cornerstone of any Cable Beach itinerary.

**Staircase to the Moon** — The optical illusion of moonlight reflecting on exposed mudflats creates an iconic celestial phenomenon. It occurs during full moons March-October when tidal conditions align. Plan timing around lunar phases; the Broome tourism office publishes exact viewing dates. The effect is visible from Cable Beach and from the low-tide mudflats near Roebuck Bay.

**Sunset Photography** — The red cliffs + white sand + golden hour light create unparalleled photography conditions. Arrive 90 minutes before sunset; bring a good camera and polarizer filter. The cliffs' warm tones deepen dramatically in final light.

**Malcolm Douglas Crocodile Park** — Located in Broome (15 km away), this facility houses saltwater crocodiles, snakes, and other Kimberley fauna. It offers guided educational tours that contextualize the wildlife you'll encounter. Entry is AUD $35–45; plan 2 hours.

**Broome Tourism** — The Broome Visitor Centre (downtown) stocks tide tables, stinger advisories, weather forecasts, and current safety information. Visit on arrival to get real-time conditions and local guidance.
