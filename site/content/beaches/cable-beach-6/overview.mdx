Cable Beach is Broome's defining 22-kilometer arc of white sand meeting distinctive red pindan cliffs—one of Western Australia's most photographed coastal stretches. Named for the 1889 telegraph cable that still runs beneath the seafloor, the beach has evolved into an internationally recognized destination, yet retains its remote Kimberley character.

The beach's defining feature is the dramatic geology: towering rust-red cliffs composed of pindan sandstone drop directly to pristine white sand, creating a landscape that intensifies at sunset when the cliffs turn deep crimson and mirror in the wet sand at low tide. The Indian Ocean frontage delivers genuine tropical conditions, with a 10-meter tidal range that exposes and submerges vast swaths of beach throughout the day.

Cable Beach is inseparable from its camel heritage. Since the 1890s, camels have worked this coast—introduced during the pearling boom and now iconic in the sunset experience. Multiple camel safari operators run daily rides at low tide and sunset, a genuinely unique Kimberley experience unavailable elsewhere in Australia.

The astronomical phenomenon adds another layer: the Staircase to the Moon occurs during full moons from March through October, when the moon's reflection creates an illusion of stairs descending into the Indian Ocean—a sight that draws photographers and travelers specifically to time their visit around lunar phases.

**Most Relevant Lenses:**

**Travel & Recreation** — Cable Beach is a destination beach requiring genuine planning: Broome is remote, accommodation is limited and expensive, and the experience depends on tide times and seasonal conditions. Sunset camel rides are the marquee activity.

**Adventure** — The 22km beach invites exploration; the northern sections accessible only by 4WD add backcountry flavor. Large tidal swings create dynamic beach conditions throughout the day.

**Photography** — Red cliffs, white sand, the Staircase to the Moon, and golden-hour camel silhouettes create some of Australia's most dramatic coastal photographs.

**Environment** — The ecosystem supports 20 documented bird species and crucial waterbird habitat. The beach also hosts populations of saltwater crocodiles and box jellyfish—hazards that require respect and seasonal awareness.

Cable Beach endures because it delivers genuine remoteness, distinctive geology, and an experience—camel rides at sunset on a 22km arc of red and white—that cannot be replicated elsewhere.
