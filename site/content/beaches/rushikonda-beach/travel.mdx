# Rushikonda Beach Travel Guide

Rushikonda Beach sits on the coastal road between Visakhapatnam and Bhimili in Andhra Pradesh, about 8 to 10 km north of the city centre. It is Visakhapatnam's main water-sports beach: golden quartz sand backed by the wooded Rushikonda hill, with jet skis, speedboats, and paramotoring working off the sand from mid-morning. It is not a surf destination—the waves are moderate—but it is the busiest and best-known beach on this stretch of coast, and India's only Blue Flag beach outside its handful of certified sites elsewhere.

## Getting There

### By Air

The nearest airport is **Visakhapatnam International Airport (VTZ)**, about 17.9 km south of the beach, with domestic flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Chennai and limited international services. The drive from VTZ to Rushikonda takes roughly 30 to 45 minutes depending on traffic. Taxis and ride-hailing (Ola, Uber) operate in the city; a taxi to Rushikonda typically runs a few hundred rupees.

### By Train

**Visakhapatnam Junction** is one of India's major rail hubs, with long-distance services from across the country (Hyderabad ~12 to 14 hours, Chennai ~13 to 16 hours, Delhi ~28 to 32 hours). The station is in the city centre, roughly 8 to 10 km south of Rushikonda; a taxi or auto-rickshaw north takes 20 to 30 minutes.

### By Road and Bus

National Highway 16 connects Visakhapatnam to the major cities of the region. Long-distance buses (many run by APSRTC, the state transport corporation) arrive at the city bus station, from which local transport reaches the beach. Within the city, **APSRTC city bus routes including 900K and 900T run from the railway station past Rushikonda**; taxis, autos, and shared autos also cover the trip.

## Best Time to Visit

**October to February** is the comfortable season: daytime temperatures generally in the mid-20s to low-30s Celsius, lower humidity, and calmer seas. This is peak tourist time, busiest on winter weekends.

**March to June** is hot and increasingly humid, with the sea warming and conditions less pleasant on the sand.

**Cyclone risk is real on this coast, concentrated in the October to December window and again around the pre-monsoon.** The Bay of Bengal is one of the most cyclone-prone basins in the world; Cyclone Hudhud made a direct hit on Visakhapatnam in October 2014. Check forecasts if travelling in the storm season.

The southwest monsoon brings heavier rain and rougher seas in the middle of the year, when water-sports operators scale back.

## Where to Stay

Rushikonda itself has limited lodging directly on the beach, with a small number of guesthouses and rentals nearby. Most visitors stay in **Visakhapatnam**, either along the city's own **Ramakrishna (RK) Beach** waterfront 8 to 10 km south—well supplied with hotels across budget and upscale tiers, more restaurants, and a lively promenade—or in the downtown area closer to the transport hubs. A taxi or auto covers the 20 to 30 minute trip to Rushikonda. Note that the APTDC complex on Rushikonda hill, built as a resort, has been closed to the public pending court proceedings, so it is not an accommodation option.

## Costs

**Beach access** is free; there is no entry fee for the sand. Expect modest parking charges at designated lots.

**Food** on the beach is limited to informal vendors (roasted corn, chaat, ice cream) and the APTDC's own facilities; fuller meals, especially Andhra seafood, are in the city.

**Water sports** are the main paid activity—jet skiing, speedboat rides, and paramotoring are booked directly with the seasonal operators on the sand, typically a few hundred to a few thousand rupees per session depending on the activity. Confirm price, duration, and safety gear before you get in.

India is inexpensive by Western standards; a comfortable day here costs a fraction of an equivalent day on a European or US coast.

## Safety

**Swim only inside the flagged zone.** Rushikonda is an open-coast beach with currents that have caused repeated drownings despite posted warnings; the city and municipal corporation deploy community guards and run a warning-flag system (green safe, yellow caution, red no swimming). Heed the flags and the lifeguards, and stay out of the water after dark, when the sea is most dangerous and unstaffed.

**Rip currents:** if caught, swim parallel to the shore until out of the pull, then back in—do not fight it directly.

**Sun and heat** are intense; carry water and sunscreen. Tap water is not for drinking—use bottled water.

**General:** don't leave valuables unattended on the sand; dress is more conservative than on Western beaches. If you rent a vehicle, Indian road conditions demand defensive driving.

**Shark risk is effectively nil**—there are no recorded shark incidents at Rushikonda.

## Nearby

Visakhapatnam rewards a few days beyond the beach. **Kailasagiri**, a hilltop park reached by a cable car, overlooks the coast; the **Kursura Submarine Museum** (the decommissioned INS Kursura) sits on RK Beach in the city; and the **Araku Valley**, a hill and coffee-growing region roughly three hours inland by the scenic Kirandul railway line, is a common day or overnight trip. Rushikonda works well as the beach half of a Visakhapatnam itinerary rather than a standalone destination.
