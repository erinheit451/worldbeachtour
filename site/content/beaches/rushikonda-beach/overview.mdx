Rushikonda Beach is a golden crescent of sand on the Bay of Bengal, on the coastal road between Visakhapatnam and Bhimili in Andhra Pradesh, India. The name is Telugu for "hill of the sages"—*rushi*, sage; *konda*, hill—after the wooded hill that rises directly behind the shore, not the Yarada hills further south. The sand is quartz-dominant, modelled at roughly 92% quartz, which is why it reads gold rather than the darker grey common along much of India's east coast. The Andhra Pradesh Tourism Development Corporation (APTDC) manages the beach.

Rushikonda is Visakhapatnam's main water-sports beach. From mid-morning, jet skis, speedboats, and paramotoring operators work off the sand; the waves themselves are moderate, averaging just over a metre through the year and biggest around July, so this is a swimming-and-motorised-sports beach rather than a surf destination. Because this is India's east coast, the sun rises out of the sea here, making dawn the beach's quiet spectacle.

In October 2020, Rushikonda became one of the first eight beaches in India to receive the **Blue Flag**, the international eco-label run by the Foundation for Environmental Education for water quality, safety, services, and coastal-zone management. It remains the only Blue Flag beach in Andhra Pradesh. The certification is an ongoing obligation, not a permanent honour: the beach lost the flag in February 2025 over maintenance and hygiene lapses, then regained it weeks later after corrective work.

The hill the beach is named for is also why Rushikonda became a national story. From 2021 the state government demolished the APTDC's old Haritha resort and built a ₹450-crore complex on the hill, marketed as a tourism resort and later identified by a government panel as a camp office and residence for the chief minister. Roughly 20 acres of the hill were cut and reshaped, and petitioners took the project to the National Green Tribunal and the High Court alleging violations of the Coastal Regulation Zone rules—the same coastal-management standard the Blue Flag certifies on the sand below. That paradox is the defining feature of the place.

**Most relevant lenses:**

**Coastal development and rules** — Rushikonda is the clearest place in India to see coastal-zone regulation applied to a shoreline and contested on the hill immediately above it. The Blue Flag, the CRZ litigation, and the disputed complex are one story, told on a single beach.

**Water sports** — The beach is the hub for Visakhapatnam's jet-ski, speedboat, and paramotoring scene, seasonal and weather-dependent, run largely off the open sand from mid-morning.

**Safety and exposure** — This is one of the world's most active cyclone coasts; Cyclone Hudhud struck Visakhapatnam directly in October 2014. Drownings recur despite guards and warning flags, so swimming is restricted to the flagged zone.

**Urban beach culture** — Rushikonda is where a major port and shipbuilding city meets the sea for recreation, backed by a hill that is now as much a courtroom exhibit as a landmark.
