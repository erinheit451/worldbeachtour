# Kollam Beach Travel Guide

Kollam Beach sits at the gateway to Kerala's backwater system and within one of South India's historic port cities. Located on the Arabian Sea coast in Kollam district, this urban beach offers a window into Kerala's maritime heritage—where fishing boats share the water with houseboat tourism and cargo vessels still operate from the colonial-era port. Whether you're visiting for the historical atmosphere, seeking passage into Ashtamudi Lake's backwaters, or simply experiencing authentic local Kerala, Kollam provides genuine cultural immersion without the resort veneer of more touristed beaches.

## Getting There

### By Air

Most international and domestic flights arrive at **Thiruvananthapuram International Airport (TRV)**, located 70 kilometers south of Kollam. This is Kerala's primary aviation gateway, with connections to major Indian cities and select international hubs. The drive from TRV to Kollam takes 1.5–2 hours via National Highway 66, following the coast northward. A taxi or Uber from the airport costs ₹1,200–1,800 (approximately $14–22 USD). Avoid traveling between 4–6 PM on weekdays when highway traffic concentrates.

A smaller secondary option is **Cochin International Airport (COK)**, 150 kilometers north of Kollam—a 3–3.5 hour drive but offers some flight alternatives. Choose this only if you have specific flight availability; it's not closer despite being Kerala's other major airport.

### By Train

**Kollam Junction Railway Station** sits on the main coastal railway line, with direct connections to most major South Indian cities. This is the most practical transport method if arriving from other parts of Kerala or South India.

- **From Thiruvananthapuram**: 60 kilometers, approximately 1.5–2 hours (express trains available daily)
- **From Cochin**: 230 kilometers, approximately 5–6 hours (multiple options daily)
- **From Bangalore**: 600+ kilometers, 10–12 hours (overnight sleeper trains)

The railway station is 3 kilometers from the beach; hire an auto-rickshaw (₹80–120 / $1–1.50) or use local buses to reach the waterfront. Train tickets are affordable (₹50–300 / $0.60–3.60 depending on class and distance) and represent authentic local travel.

### By Road

**National Highway 66** (formerly NH-17) runs directly along Kerala's coast, connecting Kollam to both Thiruvananthapuram (70 km south) and Cochin (150 km north). If driving yourself:

- **From Thiruvananthapuram**: 1.5–2 hours, straightforward NH-66 north
- **From Cochin**: 3–3.5 hours, scenic coastal drive
- **From Bangalore**: 600+ kilometers, 10–12 hours (requires overnight stop)

Kollam city has modest parking availability near the beach. Street parking is common; expect ₹30–50 (₹0.40–0.60) per hour in paid zones. The highway itself is well-maintained, though traffic can be heavy near Kollam city during peak hours (8–10 AM, 4–6 PM weekdays).

### By Bus

**KSRTC (Kerala State Road Transport Corporation)** operates long-distance buses connecting Kollam to cities across Kerala and neighboring states.

- **From Thiruvananthapuram**: Frequent services, 2–3 hours, ₹100–150
- **From Cochin**: 5–6 hour journey, multiple daily services
- **From Bangalore**: Overnight buses available, 10–12 hours

Private bus operators (VRL, SRS, Kallada) offer air-conditioned coaches with slightly higher comfort and cost. The main bus station (KSRTC stand) is 1 kilometer from the beach. Local auto-rickshaws and shared taxis bridge this gap inexpensively.

### By Water

Limited ferry services connect Kollam to other coastal towns, but no regular scheduled ferry serves the beach directly. However, **houseboat operators and water taxi services** do operate from Kollam, primarily for backwater tours (see Ashtamudi Lake below). Private boat transport to other backwater locations is available through the Kollam Tourism office.

## Best Time to Visit

### Seasonal Overview

**November–February** is the optimal season. Daytime temperatures are comfortable (28–32°C / 82–90°F), nights are cool (20–24°C / 68–75°F), and rainfall is minimal. The Arabian Sea is calm, humidity is lower, and visibility is clear. This is peak tourist season; expect more visitors but predictable weather. Early mornings are particularly pleasant for walking and photography.

**March–May** is hot and dry (32–36°C / 90–97°F). The beach experiences significant heat; early morning and evening activities are more comfortable. Humidity begins rising in April–May. Fewer tourists visit during this shoulder period. Swimming is possible but the sea is warm and sometimes turbid.

**June–September** is the southwest monsoon season. Heavy rainfall occurs daily, often intense in the afternoon. The Arabian Sea is rough with strong currents and high waves—swimming is dangerous and not recommended. Roads can flood; travel is slowest during July–August. Most tourist facilities close or operate with reduced hours. This season is primarily experienced by locals and adventurous visitors seeking the dramatic, moody landscape and lower prices.

**October** marks monsoon transition. Rain decreases toward the month's end, but the sea remains rough. The landscape is lush and green. This is an underrated time for those comfortable with sporadic rain—fewer visitors, refreshed surroundings, and manageable weather.

## Where to Stay

Kollam city center offers the most practical accommodation, with options on or near the beach waterfront. The city itself is compact and walkable; a 5–10 minute walk reaches most beach areas from central lodging.

### Kollam City (Waterfront & Central)

The most convenient base, with the beach within walking distance. Accommodation ranges from modest local hotels to heritage properties in restored colonial buildings.

- **Heritage hotels**: ₹2,000–4,000 / $24–48 per night; housed in restored merchant houses and colonial-era buildings with character and Kollam history
- **Mid-range hotels**: ₹1,200–2,000 / $14–24 per night; basic but clean, air-conditioned, with restaurants
- **Budget hotels**: ₹600–1,000 / $7–12 per night; functional rooms, no frills, excellent value
- **Guesthouses and homestays**: ₹800–1,500 / $10–18 per night; local family operations with personality and personalized attention

### Thangasseri Area

3 kilometers north along the coast, Thangasseri is a quieter neighborhood with views of the historic lighthouse and access to less-crowded beach sections. A mix of vacation rentals and small guesthouses serve visitors seeking more atmosphere than central Kollam.

- **Vacation rentals**: ₹1,500–2,500 / $18–30 per night for 1–2 bedrooms
- **Small hotels**: ₹1,000–1,800 / $12–22 per night

### Ashtamudi Lake Houseboats

The iconic Kerala backwater experience launches from Kollam. Traditional wooden houseboats (kettuvallams) carry overnight guests through the backwater channels, with meals and all-day guided tours included.

- **Budget houseboats**: ₹4,000–6,000 / $48–72 per night per couple; shared boats with basic amenities
- **Deluxe houseboats**: ₹8,000–15,000 / $96–180 per night per couple; private boats with air-conditioning and en-suite facilities
- **Luxury houseboats**: ₹15,000–25,000+ / $180–300+ per night; high-end private vessels with extensive service

Houseboat tours typically run 24–36 hours and include all meals, navigation, and backwater experience. Book through licensed operators; many hotels can arrange directly.

### Nearby Alternatives (If Kollam is Full)

- **Pathanamthitta** (45 km north): Gateway to other backwater systems; 1.5 hours by road
- **Thiruvananthapuram** (70 km south): Larger city with more hotel chains; 1.5–2 hours away

Book accommodation at least 2–3 weeks ahead during peak season (December–January). Monsoon and shoulder seasons (June–September, October) offer last-minute availability and discounted rates.

## Costs

### General Price Level

Kollam is remarkably affordable by international standards. A modest daily budget of ₹1,500–2,000 ($18–24) covers accommodation, meals, and local transport for budget travelers. Mid-range visitors spend ₹3,000–5,000 ($36–60). Premium travel stays ₹7,000–10,000+ ($84–120+).

### Breakdown

**Food and beverages**:
- Street food (idli, dosa, vadai, fresh fish curry): ₹50–150 / $0.60–1.80
- Casual local restaurants: ₹150–300 / $1.80–3.60 per meal
- Tourist-oriented restaurants: ₹400–700 / $4.80–8.40 per meal
- Higher-end dining: ₹700–1,500 / $8.40–18 per entree

**Accommodation**: ₹600–4,000+ per night (see Where to Stay above)

**Transport**:
- Auto-rickshaw within Kollam: ₹50–100 / $0.60–1.20
- Taxi to/from TRV airport: ₹1,200–1,800 / $14–22
- Train fare (Thiruvananthapuram to Kollam): ₹100–200 / $1.20–2.40
- Local buses: ₹15–50 / $0.20–0.60

**Activities**:
- Ashtamudi Lake houseboat tour (24-hour): ₹4,000–15,000 / $48–180 per couple
- Thangasseri Lighthouse visit: Free (local area access)
- Oachira temple visit: Free
- Fishing village boat tours: ₹200–500 / $2.40–6 per person

**Entrance and fees**: Most public areas are free. Museum and heritage site fees typically ₹20–50 / $0.25–0.60.

**Estimated daily budgets**:
- **Shoestring**: ₹1,200–1,500 / $14–18 (street food, budget hotel, local buses)
- **Mid-range**: ₹3,000–4,000 / $36–48 (local restaurants, modest hotel, occasional taxis)
- **Comfortable**: ₹6,000–10,000 / $72–120 (good restaurants, quality hotel, houseboat day-tour)

## Safety

### Swimming and Water Conditions

The Arabian Sea at Kollam requires caution:

- **Strong currents**: The coast experiences significant water movement; swimming should only occur during daylight in designated areas
- **Waves**: During monsoon (June–September), waves are rough and dangerous; swimming is not recommended. November–March offers much calmer conditions
- **Visibility**: Water clarity varies; it's generally better during dry season (November–February) and reduces during monsoon
- **No lifeguards**: No formal lifeguard stations are present; swim at your own risk and use the buddy system
- **Sharks**: Zero recorded incidents at Kollam Beach; shark risk is minimal
- **Water quality**: The harbor environment means occasional floating debris and varying water conditions. Check with locals before swimming

**Best practice**: Swim only during calm season (November–February) in daylight, avoid immediate post-monsoon periods, and ask hotel staff or local fishermen about current water conditions.

### General Safety and Security

Kollam city is safe for travelers. The beach and waterfront areas are generally secure during daylight and early evening. Normal urban precautions apply:

- Avoid unlit areas after dark
- Don't leave valuables unattended
- Petty theft from unattended belongings is possible but uncommon
- The port area (immediate waterfront) is busier and safer than isolated stretches

Political rallies and religious processions occasionally occur; they're peaceful but can cause traffic delays. Ask your hotel about local events before planning transport.

### Health Considerations

- **Water-borne illness**: Tap water is not reliably safe; drink bottled or filtered water
- **Monsoon-related issues**: During June–September, waterborne diseases increase; maintain strict water/food safety
- **Sunburn**: The sun is strong year-round; apply sunscreen regularly
- **Heat exhaustion**: May–June temperatures can exceed 35°C; carry water and rest during midday heat
- **Insects**: Mosquitoes are present; use repellent, especially during monsoon season

## Local Tips

**Explore Thangasseri Lighthouse.** 3 kilometers north of the main beach, this 200-year-old colonial lighthouse is accessible during daylight. The area around it has restored heritage buildings and quieter beach sections ideal for photography and walking.

**Experience Ashtamudi Lake by houseboat.** This is Kollam's primary attraction—a multi-hour or overnight journey through Kerala's famous backwater system. Houseboats launch daily; booking through your hotel or a licensed operator is straightforward. Early morning departures offer better light and fewer other boats.

**Visit Oachira Temple.** 10 kilometers north, this ancient temple with backwater island location is historically significant and culturally authentic. Local buses serve it; allow half a day.

**Fish at dawn.** Watch traditional fishing operations from the beach at sunrise. Fishermen are accustomed to visitors; a polite request for photographs is usually welcomed. The light is spectacular.

**Eat fresh fish.** Kollam's coastal location means exceptional seafood. Request grilled kingfish (meen) or prawns at local restaurants; preparation is simple and the quality is outstanding.

**Hire a local fisherman.** Individual fishing boat operators offer morning or afternoon excursions for ₹300–500 per person. These are less touristy than commercial houseboat tours and provide genuine local perspective.

**Walk the waterfront at sunset.** The promenade along Kollam beach offers evening breezes and atmospheric light; locals gather here after the heat of the day.

**Take a backwater canoe ride.** Kayaks and traditional canoes are available for rent (₹500–800 / $6–10 per hour) through local operators. Paddling narrow waterways offers a quieter, more intimate experience than large houseboats.

**Visit during festival season.** October–December brings local festivals, temple processions, and cultural events. If present during these times, ask your hotel about attending—they're accessible and authentic.

**Learn local Malayalam phrases.** "Namaskaram" (hello), "Shukriyam" (thank you), and "Evare?" (how are you?) go a long way. Locals appreciate effort.

**Shop at local markets.** Morning markets (Kollam Central Market) sell fresh produce, spices, and Kerala specialties. These offer genuine local culture away from tourist areas.
