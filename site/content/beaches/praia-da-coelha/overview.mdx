Praia da Coelha is a small, sheltered sandy cove nestled between ochre-colored cliffs on Portugal's Algarve coast, located west of central Albufeira. This intimate beach offers a quieter alternative to the busier central Albufeira waterfront, providing both geological intrigue and a sense of privacy that larger, more developed beaches in the region cannot match.

The defining visual feature is the dramatic formation of rust-colored cliff faces that embrace the cove, creating a natural amphitheater effect. These ochre walls are the Algarve's geological signature—compressed layers of iron-rich sedimentary rock that weather and shift with the Atlantic swells, giving the landscape a distinctive warm palette year-round. Between the cliffs runs a relatively narrow band of golden sand, seldom crowded even during peak summer months, making it a preferred spot for visitors seeking the Algarve experience without the throngs.

The name itself—"Rabbit Beach" in Portuguese—has uncertain origins; local speculation suggests either historical rabbit populations or a metaphorical reference to something small and hidden away. The beach sits just 4 kilometers west of Albufeira proper, making it accessible enough for day visitors but secluded enough to feel like a minor discovery.

Water quality is excellent. The beach carries an EU "excellent" bathing water rating, supported by strong circulation from the open Atlantic. The sandy substrate is comfortable underfoot and typical of the region. The cove's sheltered position means gentler swell compared to more exposed stretches of coast, though swells still arrive regularly enough for body surfing and casual swimming.

**Most Relevant Lenses:**

**Nature & Geology** — The ochre cliffs form the primary appeal. This is a landscape of exposed geological time; the rust coloring and stratification tell a story of depositional history and coastal erosion that makes geology students and photography enthusiasts equally fascinated.

**Quiet Beach** — Praia da Coelha is fundamentally a refuge from Albufeira's development. The limited parking, narrow access path, and lack of beach bars or water sports operators keep crowds organic and sparse. This is where locals go when they want their beach quiet.

**Swimming & Water Quality** — The excellent EU bathing rating and sheltered position create reliable, safe swimming. This is not a rough beach; it's a genuinely pleasant place to enter the water and stay comfortable.

**Travel & Discovery** — The beach rewards those who venture slightly west from Albufeira's tourist infrastructure. It's discoverable but not accidental—you need to navigate the coast path or take a short drive—making it a spot for intentional visitors rather than chance arrivals.

**Photography** — The ochre cliffs framing golden sand and blue water create classic composition opportunities, particularly in golden-hour light when the cliff colors intensify. Wide-angle and telephoto perspectives both work equally well.

Praia da Coelha succeeds by being what its neighbors are not: a quiet, visually distinctive cove where the primary attraction is the geology and the relative absence of commercial infrastructure. It's accessible but not overdeveloped, scenic but not famous, and safe but not lifeguard-staffed. For visitors to the Algarve willing to explore slightly beyond the standard beach itinerary, it repays the detour.
