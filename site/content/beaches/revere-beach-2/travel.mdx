# Revere Beach Travel Guide

Revere Beach stands as America's first public beach, a historic oceanfront destination just minutes from downtown Boston. This accessible, no-frills Massachusetts gem offers sandy shores, classic amusement attractions, and views of the Atlantic with the Boston skyline as your backdrop. Whether you're a local day-tripper or traveling from afar, Revere Beach delivers straightforward coastal fun without pretense.

## Getting There

**By Air**

Boston Logan International Airport (BOS) is your primary gateway, located just 5.3 kilometers (3.3 miles) north of Revere Beach. Direct flights arrive from major US hubs and international cities. From Logan, you have several options:

- **Rental car**: 15–20 minutes via I-90 W and Route 1A North, depending on traffic. Parking at Revere Beach is metered but abundant along the boulevard; expect $1.50–$2.00 per hour.
- **Public transit**: The MBTA Blue Line runs directly from Logan to Revere Beach Station (Revere stop) in approximately 20 minutes. This is the fastest, most economical option ($2.40 one-way).
- **Rideshare**: Uber/Lyft typically cost $12–$18 from the airport.

**By Car**

If driving from elsewhere in New England:
- **From New York City** (215 km): Take I-95 North toward Boston, merge onto Route 1 North; approximately 3.5 hours.
- **From Portland, Maine** (105 km): I-295 South to I-95 South toward Boston, then Route 1 North; approximately 2 hours.
- **From Providence, Rhode Island** (85 km): I-95 North toward Boston, exit onto Route 1 North; approximately 1.5 hours.

Revere Beach Boulevard (Route 1A) runs directly along the shore and is the main access road. Street parking fills quickly on summer weekends; arrive early or use the municipal lots near the Wonderland amusement park.

**By Public Transit**

Beyond the MBTA Blue Line, the Orange Line serves nearby Sullivan Square Station, with bus connections to Revere Beach Boulevard. The Green Line reaches downtown Boston, where the Blue Line can be accessed for the final leg to the beach. Regional MBTA commuter rail also connects to Revere Station on the Newburyport/Rockport Line.

**Nearest City**

Revere (population ~51,000) is the beach's immediate urban center, just 1.7 kilometers inland. Hotels, restaurants, and services cluster along Ocean Avenue and American Legion Highway.

## Best Time to Visit

**Summer (June–August)**

Peak season brings warm water (65–70°F / 18–21°C) and air temperatures of 75–85°F (24–29°C). July and August are busiest; expect crowds, especially on weekends. Water quality is generally good, and lifeguards are on duty daily. The famous Revere Beach Surf Film Festival typically happens in August.

**Spring (April–May)**

Shoulder season offers mild weather (55–65°F / 13–18°C) and far fewer crowds. Water remains cold (45–55°F / 7–13°C), limiting swimming comfort. Beaches are clean after winter, and local businesses reopen. Ideal for walks and photography.

**Fall (September–October)**

Early September still captures summer warmth (70–75°F / 21–24°C) with noticeably lighter crowds. By October, air temperatures drop to 50–60°F (10–15°C) and water becomes frigid (55–60°F / 13–15°C). Autumn light is exceptional for photography. Many seasonal vendors close by late September.

**Winter (November–March)**

Harsh Atlantic winds, water temps below 45°F (7°C), and frequent closures make winter the low season. However, the beach is never truly crowded, and solitary walks offer contemplative beauty. The Revere Beach Winterfest (February) brings cultural programming and events.

## Where to Stay

**Revere & Beachfront**

The beachfront boulevard features older motor inns, mid-range chains, and some boutique properties. This area puts you steps from sand and restaurants but can be noisier, especially near the amusement park. Summer weekends book months ahead.

**Nearby Wonderland Neighborhood**

South of the main beach, Wonderland offers quieter residential streets with easier parking and proximity to the Blue Line. Accommodations here are slightly cheaper and better for families seeking a less carnival-like vibe.

**East Boston (Adjacent)**

Across the Revere/East Boston border, you'll find lower prices and excellent Blue Line access. This neighborhood has undergone recent revitalization with new restaurants and shops, making it an increasingly appealing base.

**Downtown Boston (10 km Away)**

If you want more dining and nightlife variety, stay downtown and use the 20-minute MBTA commute. Premium pricing but unmatched urban amenities.

**Accommodation Types**

- **Hotels & Motor Inns**: Budget-friendly chains dominate; expect $80–$150/night off-season, $140–$220/night in summer.
- **Short-term rentals**: Airbnb and VRBO options range from studio apartments ($70–$110/night) to larger units ($150–$300/night).
- **Vacation rentals**: Some landlords rent seasonal cottages; contact the Revere Chamber of Commerce for listings.

## Safety

**Swimming Conditions**

Revere Beach is a strong-current ocean beach. Lifeguards patrol the main strand from May through September. Swimming is safest in designated lifeguarded areas during official hours (typically 9 AM–5:30 PM). The Atlantic here is cold year-round; wetsuits are recommended even in summer for extended time in water.

**Shark Incidents**

Revere Beach has recorded **zero shark incidents** on record, making it exceptionally safe from marine predators. Great whites and other species may pass offshore, but attacks are virtually unheard of here.

**Water Quality**

The Massachusetts Department of Public Health monitors water quality regularly. Revere Beach occasionally closes temporarily after heavy rainfall due to stormwater runoff. Check the state's swimming advisory website before visiting. Winter months see fewer testing events and occasional temporary closures.

**Crime & General Safety**

Revere has a mixed reputation; the beachfront and main boulevard are generally safe and well-patrolled, especially in daylight. Avoid isolated areas after dark. Keep valuables out of sight in your car. The area is improving steadily with new development and community policing efforts.

**Health Considerations**

- **Sun exposure**: The Atlantic provides less UV protection than inland areas. Strong UV index in summer; bring SPF 30+ sunscreen.
- **Rip currents**: Swim near lifeguards and heed their warnings. If caught in a current, swim parallel to shore, not against it.
- **Facilities**: Public restrooms, showers, and changing facilities are available near the main lifeguard stations.

## Local Tips

**Arrive Early in Summer**

Parking fills by 11 AM on sunny weekends. Aim for 9–10 AM or plan to stay into early evening when crowds thin.

**The Greenway Connection**

A paved path runs the length of Revere Beach Boulevard, perfect for jogging, cycling, or casual strolling. Rent bikes locally for a scenic alternative to driving.

**Seasonal Events**

Check the Revere Chamber of Commerce calendar. The Revere Beach International Sand Sculpting Festival (July) and Revere Beach Winterfest (February) draw crowds and offer free entertainment.

**Food Beyond the Boardwalk**

Walk inland one block to Ocean Avenue and American Legion Highway for authentic Italian restaurants, family-owned pizza joints, and ethnic eateries frequented by locals. Prices are 30–40% lower than boardwalk vendors.

**Sunset Views**

The beach faces northeast, not west, so sunrises over the water are the show here—arrive at 5:30–6:00 AM in summer for a quiet, golden-light experience.

**MBTA Strategy**

If visiting from Boston, buy a day pass ($11) for unlimited transit instead of round-trip tickets. The Blue Line drops you within a 5-minute walk of the main strand.

**Wildlife Watching**

The beach is a migratory corridor for seabirds. Over 20 species have been recorded here, including Great Black-backed Gulls, American Herring Gulls, and seasonal songbirds. Binoculars and a bird app enhance your visit.

**Winter Solitude**

If crowds exhaust you, return in November or February. You'll have the beach nearly to yourself, the light is beautiful, and locals are friendly and less harried.
