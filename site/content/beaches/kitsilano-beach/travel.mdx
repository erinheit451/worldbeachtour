# Kitsilano Beach Travel Guide

Kitsilano Beach, known locally as Kits Beach, sits on the south shore of English Bay on Vancouver's west side, facing north across the water to the downtown peninsula and the North Shore mountains. It is an urban swimming beach with a 137-metre heated saltwater pool, banks of volleyball courts, a seawall path, and the free Kitsilano Showboat stage. Its east end runs up against Kitsilano Indian Reserve No. 6, the Squamish village of Sen̓áḵw, where the nation's Sen̓áḵw towers are under construction beside the Burrard Bridge.

## Getting There

### By Air

The nearest airport is **Vancouver International Airport (YVR)**, on Sea Island about 9.3 kilometres south of the beach. By car the trip takes roughly 20–30 minutes outside of the weekday rush (about 7–9 AM and 4–6 PM). From YVR, take Highway 99 north across the Arthur Laing Bridge and work toward Kitsilano via Granville Street and West 4th Avenue or Cornwall Avenue.

By transit from YVR, ride the **Canada Line** train toward downtown and transfer to a westbound bus into Kitsilano. Trips starting at the airport carry a **$6.50 YVR AddFare** on top of the regular fare, so the airport leg runs roughly $9–10 for an adult; a taxi or ride-share from YVR is usually about $30–45 CAD.

### By Car

**From Seattle, Washington** (about 230 kilometres): take I-5 north to the Peace Arch / Blaine border crossing, where the highway becomes BC Highway 99 into Vancouver. Plan 3–4 hours plus border-wait time.

**From Whistler** (about 125 kilometres north): take BC Highway 99 (the Sea-to-Sky Highway) south into Vancouver; plan 1.5–2 hours.

**From Victoria, BC**: ride BC Ferries from Swartz Bay to Tsawwassen (about 95 minutes on the water), then follow BC-17 and BC-99 into the city. Budget 3–3.5 hours in total, including the ferry and possible sailing waits.

Street parking around Cornwall Avenue, West 4th Avenue, and the residential blocks behind the waterfront is free but limited, and there is a metered pay lot off Cornwall by the pool. On sunny summer weekends (July–August), arrive by mid-morning to park near the sand.

### By Public Transit

**TransLink** buses serve the beach directly. Useful routes include the **#2** (Burrard Station–Macdonald, running past the beach on Cornwall Avenue), the **#4** and **#7** along West 4th Avenue and Cornwall, and the limited-stop **#84** on West 4th during the day. The closest stops are on Cornwall Avenue a short walk from the sand. As of July 2026 a single adult fare is **$3.50 CAD** in cash or **$2.85** with stored value on a Compass Card; check TransLink for current pricing.

### By Ferry

**False Creek Ferries** runs small passenger boats inside False Creek and stops at the **Maritime Museum dock in Vanier Park**, a short walk from the beach's east end. (The other operator, Aquabus, does not serve the Vanier Park side.) The ferries connect to Granville Island, Yaletown, and Science World, but they do not land on Kits Beach itself, which faces English Bay outside the creek.

## Best Time to Visit

**Summer (June–August)** is the peak. Daytime highs commonly sit in the low-to-mid 20s Celsius (70s Fahrenheit) and daylight runs past 9 PM. This is the window for the outdoor pool and for swimming in the bay, though the water stays cool, generally 15–17°C (59–63°F). Expect crowds on sunny weekends.

**Spring (April–May)** and **fall (September–October)** are the shoulder seasons: milder, less crowded, and good for the seawall and the neighbourhood. September often holds summer warmth with fewer people, and the bay keeps some residual heat before the autumn rain sets in.

**Winter (November–March)** is mild by Canadian standards (roughly 3–8°C) but wet and grey. The outdoor pool is closed for the season and the beach is quiet.

### Pool Season

**Kitsilano Pool** is a seasonal outdoor pool, open roughly from the Victoria Day long weekend in late May to early September, weather and repairs permitting. A January 2022 storm and king tide damaged the seaside structure, and after repairs it has opened late in recent years — on 7 August in 2024 and in late June in 2025. Adult drop-in admission is about **$8 CAD**, with a 50% discount for swimmers arriving in the final 45 minutes of a session. Because opening dates, hours, and any time-slot rules shift year to year, confirm current details with the City of Vancouver / Park Board before relying on the pool.

## Where to Stay

Kitsilano is mostly a low-rise residential neighbourhood, so lodging on the beach itself is limited. Most visitors stay in one of three areas; rather than name specific properties, here is the honest lay of the land.

### Kitsilano

Staying in Kitsilano puts you within walking distance of the sand and the shops, cafes, and restaurants along West 4th Avenue and Cornwall Avenue. Accommodation here is mostly short-term vacation rentals and a small number of guesthouses rather than large hotels; supply is thin, so book well ahead.

### West End

Immediately across the Burrard Bridge, the West End is denser and more urban, with a wider range of hotels and price points, and it is a 10–15 minute transit ride or a longer walk to the beach.

### Downtown Vancouver

Many visitors base themselves downtown (near the waterfront, Yaletown, or Gastown) for the widest choice of hotels, dining, and attractions, then day-trip to Kits in about 15–30 minutes by transit.

Availability and rates are tightest in July and August and ease from September onward.

## Costs

Kitsilano Beach itself is **free to access** — no entrance fee, and street parking is free where you can find it.

- **Kitsilano Pool**: about $8 CAD per adult drop-in (50% off in the last 45 minutes of a session).
- **Food and drink**: cafes and restaurants along West 4th Avenue run from casual lunches (about $12–20 CAD) to higher-end dinners; the seasonal beach concession serves fried chicken, beer, and cocktails on the sand.
- **Beach rentals**: paddleboard and kayak rentals operate seasonally from independent outfitters, roughly $30–50 CAD per hour.
- **Transport**: a single adult bus fare is $3.50 CAD (cash) or $2.85 (Compass stored value); ride-share from YVR runs about $30–45 CAD, and from downtown about $10–18 CAD.

Overall, budget roughly $60–100 CAD per person for a day visit, more if you stay overnight and eat out.

## Safety

### Swimming Conditions

- **Water temperature** ranges from single digits Celsius in winter to about 15–17°C (59–63°F) at the height of summer; the bay is cold enough that long swims are chilly without a wetsuit.
- **Tides**: Vancouver's tidal range can exceed 4 metres, so the beach is much wider at low tide and narrows sharply at high tide. Check a tide table before visiting.
- **Waves and currents**: English Bay is sheltered, and wave heights average well under half a metre, so conditions are usually calm rather than surf.
- **Lifeguards**: the beach is patrolled by Vancouver Park Board lifeguards through the summer swimming season, and the pool is lifeguarded whenever it is open. Outside those hours there is no lifeguard on duty.
- **Water quality**: Vancouver Coastal Health monitors the bay and posts E. coli advisories, usually after heavy rain, when bacteria pass safe limits. Check current beach advisories before swimming, and avoid swimming after dark.

### General Safety

Kitsilano is a populated, generally secure neighbourhood, and the beach stays busy into the evening. Break-ins to parked cars do happen, so keep valuables out of sight and off the sand. Vancouver's summer sun is stronger than it feels, especially reflecting off the water, so bring sunscreen and a layer for the cool mornings and evenings.

## Nearby

- **Vanier Park**, adjacent to the beach's east end, holds the Vancouver Maritime Museum, the Museum of Vancouver, and the H.R. MacMillan Space Centre planetarium.
- The **seawall path** links Kits Beach east toward Vanier Park and the Burrard Bridge and, across False Creek, connects to Granville Island and downtown by foot, bike, or ferry.
- The **east end and the Sen̓áḵw site** at the foot of the Burrard Bridge are where the Squamish Nation's towers rise on the restored reserve; the quieter sand there is the place to read the beach's history, covered in the [full Kitsilano Beach guide](/beaches/kitsilano-beach).
- **Granville Island**, a short ferry or seawall trip away, has a public market, food stalls, and craft studios.
