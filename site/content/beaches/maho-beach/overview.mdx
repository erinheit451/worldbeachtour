Maho Beach is one of the Caribbean's most electrifying and unconventional beach experiences, famous for something you won't find anywhere else: commercial aircraft landing directly over the sand. Located on the Dutch side of Saint Martin, just 1.3 kilometers from Princess Juliana International Airport, this beach has become a pilgrimage site for aviation enthusiasts, thrill-seekers, and photographers who come specifically to witness jets touching down mere meters away.

## The Unique Appeal

What transforms Maho Beach from a simple sandy shore into an international phenomenon is its proximity to the airport runway. Large commercial aircraft—Boeing 747s, Airbuses, and regional jets—make their final approach directly over the beach, creating an extraordinary sensory experience. The roar of engines, the heat from jet blasts, and the sight of a massive aircraft seemingly skimming the water creates moments that are both thrilling and utterly surreal. It's the kind of experience that dominates travel photography and viral social media content, and it's 100% real and accessible.

## Location & Surroundings

Maho Beach sits on the northwestern coast of Saint Martin, approximately 5 kilometers from the charming town of Marigot. The beach is nestled in a developed area with beachfront bars and restaurants that have capitalized on the airport activity—many visitors grab a drink at one of the beach shacks, time their arrival for incoming flights, and experience the spectacle while enjoying Caribbean hospitality.

The beach itself is a sandy crescent backed by modest development. The sand is fine and golden, the water is clear and warm, and on quieter moments between flights, it functions as a legitimate swimming and relaxation destination.

## What to Expect

Plan your visit around flight schedules rather than traditional beach time. The beach is most exciting during peak flight hours (typically afternoon and early evening), when larger international aircraft land more frequently. Between flights, it's a pleasant, if somewhat touristy, beach experience. The water is generally calm and suitable for swimming, though the primary draw isn't sun-bathing—it's the aviation spectacle and the energy of a crowd united in anticipation.

## Relevant Lenses for Your Visit

**Photography** is arguably the primary lens here. Professional and casual photographers come to capture the dramatic moment when aircraft make their approach. **Travel** matters too—this is bucket-list territory for those seeking unusual, Instagram-worthy experiences. The **history** angle is worth considering: the airport's position so close to the beach is a quirk of Caribbean geography and Caribbean practicality. For **families**, it can be entertaining (kids find the noise and scale thrilling), though it's more spectacle than traditional family beach day.

This isn't a beach for escaping crowds or finding solitude—it's a beach for experiencing something genuinely different.
