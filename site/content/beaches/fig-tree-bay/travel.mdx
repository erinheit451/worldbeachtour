# Fig Tree Bay Travel Guide

Fig Tree Bay is a 500-metre family beach in Protaras, on the eastern coast of Cyprus in the Paralimni municipality of the Ammochostos (Famagusta) district. The sand is backed by a continuous strip of hotels and tavernas, with a low uninhabited islet just offshore. The bay is sheltered, the water shallow and clear, and the beach carries Blue Flag certification. Getting there is straightforward; this guide covers how to arrive, when to go, where to stay, what it costs, and what to know about safety.

## Getting There

### By Air

Most visitors fly into **Larnaca International Airport (LCA)**, about 70 kilometres southwest of Fig Tree Bay. Larnaca is Cyprus's main international airport, with scheduled flights from across Europe and the Middle East. The drive takes roughly 45 to 60 minutes: from LCA, take the A3 motorway east toward Agia Napa and Paralimni, exit for Paralimni, and follow signs to Protaras.

**Paphos International Airport (PFO)** is the alternative, about 130 kilometres west, adding 90 or more minutes of driving. It is worth considering only if the rest of your trip is based on the western side of the island.

Note that the nearest airfield by straight-line distance sits in the Turkish-occupied north (Geçitkale) and is not usable by visitors to the Republic-controlled coast. Plan around Larnaca or Paphos.

### By Car

From elsewhere in the Republic of Cyprus:

- **From Nicosia** (about 85 km): take the A3 motorway toward Agia Napa/Paralimni. Plan roughly 1 hour 15 minutes.
- **From Limassol** (about 105 km): take the A1 east to Larnaca, then the A3 toward Paralimni. Plan roughly 1 hour 30 minutes.
- **From Paphos** (about 170 km): take the A6/A1 east past Limassol and Larnaca, then the A3. Plan roughly 2 hours 30 minutes.

A municipal car park serves the beach, and parking is free. During July and August weekends the nearest spaces fill by mid-morning; arriving early or accepting a short walk from a further lot is the trade-off in peak season.

### By Public Transit

Public buses in the Famagusta district are run by **OSEA** (the Famagusta District Transport Organisation) — not EMEL, which operates in Limassol. OSEA serves Ayia Napa, Protaras, Pernera, Paralimni, and surrounding villages. The routes most useful for reaching Fig Tree Bay are those running the Protaras coast, including the **101/102 corridor** linking Paralimni, Protaras, Pernera, Cape Greco, and Ayia Napa. Service is frequent in summer and thinner in winter; check current timetables at osea.com.cy or with hotel reception, and note that tickets are paid in cash to the driver (no cards on board).

Intercity coaches (a separate operator) connect Larnaca and other cities to the Paralimni/Ayia Napa area, where you transfer to a local OSEA route.

### By Taxi

Cyprus has no Uber. Licensed taxis are the standard door-to-door option, booked by phone, through hotel reception, or via the Bolt app where available. A taxi from Larnaca Airport to the Protaras area runs roughly €60–80 depending on time of day; agree the fare or confirm the meter before setting off.

## Best Time to Visit

The beach season runs April to October, when lifeguards are on duty and water-sports operators are active. Within that window:

**Summer (June–August)** is peak. Daytime air temperatures reach the low-to-mid 30s °C, rain is essentially absent, and sea temperatures climb toward 27°C — warm swimming without a wetsuit. Crowds are heaviest in July and August, and the small bay fills with sunbeds by mid-morning.

**Late spring (May) and early autumn (September)** offer warm water, high sunshine, and thinner crowds than mid-summer. These are the most comfortable months for combining swimming with walking and sightseeing.

**October** stays swimmable but cooler, with fewer visitors and the occasional shower as the season winds down.

**Winter (November–March)** is cool and wetter, sea temperatures fall toward the mid-teens °C, and the resort strip largely shuts. Lifeguard coverage ends outside the April–October window, so winter swimming is at your own risk.

Modelled waves in the bay are modest — an annual mean near 0.67 metres, largest in January — which is part of why the water stays calm through the swimming season.

## Where to Stay

Protaras is a linear resort town strung along several kilometres of coast, and Fig Tree Bay sits roughly in the middle of it. Rather than name specific hotels, here is the honest lay of the land:

**In Protaras (walking distance to the beach):** the widest choice, from full-service resort hotels with pools and restaurants to self-catering apartments and vacation rentals. Staying here puts Fig Tree Bay within a 5–15 minute walk, and the seafront promenade, tavernas, and shops are on your doorstep. This is the most convenient and the most expensive option, and it books up 4–6 weeks ahead for July and August.

**In Paralimni (a few kilometres inland):** the larger year-round town, with accommodation typically cheaper than the beachfront strip, better grocery shopping, and tavernas used by residents. It is a short drive or bus ride to the beach.

**Nearby resort alternatives:** Pernera, just up the coast, is quieter and family-oriented; Ayia Napa, to the south, is more built-up with a well-known nightlife scene and a busier feel. Both are within a short drive.

Prices swing sharply by season. Booking directly or through a major platform, and comparing across Protaras and Paralimni, is the practical way to gauge current rates.

## Costs

**Beach access:** free. There is no entrance fee, and the municipal car park is free.

**Sunbeds and umbrellas:** rented on the sand in season for a modest daily fee; bringing your own towel avoids it.

**Food and drink:** beach kiosks sell frappés, cold drinks, ice cream, and sandwiches. Sit-down meals are in the town behind the beach — casual tavernas for a light lunch, up to pricier seafront restaurants charging resort prices for fish and a view. A supermarket picnic is the cheapest route.

**Water-sports and rentals:** operators run water-skiing, windsurfing, parasailing, and paddleboard/snorkel hire off the bay in season, priced by the session or the day.

**Buses:** OSEA single fares are €2 for a daytime journey (04:00–21:00) and €3 at night; a 1-day pass is €6, with multi-day passes available. Cash only on board.

**Taxi from Larnaca:** roughly €60–80.

## Safety

Fig Tree Bay is one of Cyprus's more reassuring beaches for families and cautious swimmers, for concrete reasons:

- **Lifeguards** are on duty **9am to 6pm, April to October**. Outside those hours and months there is no coverage — swim accordingly.
- **Shallow, gently shelving water.** The bay slopes gradually and stays shallow well out from the sand, which is the main reason it suits young children. It is not a deep-water beach in the swimming zone.
- **Calm, sheltered conditions.** The bay is protected and waves are modest (annual mean near 0.67 m). There are no notable currents in normal conditions, and the Mediterranean here is effectively tideless.
- **High water clarity** in the sheltered shallows makes the seabed easy to see across the swimming area.
- **Blue Flag certification** covers water quality, safety provision, and management — a signal the beach is actively maintained rather than that it is wild.

Standard cautions still apply: watch for water-sports boat traffic when swimming out toward the islet, don't leave valuables unattended on the sand or in a parked car, and treat the midday Mediterranean sun seriously with shade and sunscreen. After heavy rain, water quality at any beach can dip temporarily; local operators will flag it if conditions warrant care.

## Nearby

**Cape Greco (Cavo Greco)**, a few kilometres south, is the national forest park at the southeastern tip of the island — sea caves, cliff paths, and Mediterranean scrub, and the nearest stretch of undeveloped coast to the resort strip. A half-day at Fig Tree Bay pairs naturally with a walk there.

**The offshore islet** is reachable from the beach by wading and swimming through shallow water; it is the bay's main natural focal point and an easy, low-risk snorkelling spot around the rocks. Cross with care for boat traffic.

**Pernera** (just north) and **Ayia Napa** (to the south) round out the options for a change of beach or a livelier evening.
