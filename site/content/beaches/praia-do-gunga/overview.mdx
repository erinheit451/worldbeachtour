Praia do Gunga is one of Brazil's most photographed beaches—a postcard-perfect stretch of sand that occupies a remarkable geographic position between the Atlantic Ocean and the mouth of Rio São Miguel in Alagoas. Located in Roteiro, roughly 35 kilometers south of Maceió, this slender peninsula of coconut palms and golden sand has become iconic in Brazilian beach culture, celebrated for its natural beauty and the remarkable interplay of two water systems meeting at its shore.

The beach's defining characteristic is its geography. Praia do Gunga extends as a tongue of sand separating the open Atlantic from the calmer estuary of Rio São Miguel, creating dramatically different conditions on either side. The ocean-facing shore experiences Atlantic swells and currents typical of Brazil's northeastern coast, while the estuary side offers gentler, warmer waters protected from wind and wave action. This geographic duality makes Gunga genuinely unique—you can experience two entirely different beach environments in the same location.

What draws visitors to Gunga is the complete sensory experience. The coconut palms create a lush, tropical canopy that frames the white sand and turquoise water. At low tide, natural pools form on the exposed sand flats—shallow, crystalline lagoons where you can wade and swim in water so clear that seabirds overhead are visible from below the surface. This tidal phenomenon transforms the beach morphology daily, creating new geography and new experiences with each tide cycle.

The landscape supports surprising biodiversity. Nine bird species are regularly observed, including Semipalmated Plovers and Snowy Egrets that use the shallow waters as hunting grounds. Crustaceans and mollusks populate the sand and tidal zone, supporting a small ecosystem that serves as both nursery and feeding ground for seasonal migrations.

**Most Relevant Lenses:**

**Travel & Recreation** — Praia do Gunga is a destination beach that rewards extended exploration. The tidal cycles create new experiences multiple times daily; visiting at different tides reveals entirely different beaches.

**Photography** — The visual perfection of coconut palms meeting turquoise water with the mainland receding into distance is what made Gunga iconic in Brazilian photography. Golden hour here is genuinely extraordinary.

**Adventure** — Buggy tours explore the peninsula and surrounding dunes, while jangada sailing offers authentic traditional boating. Low-tide natural pool exploration is accessible adventure for all ages.

**Environment** — The estuary ecology, tidal zone biodiversity, and the interplay of freshwater and saltwater create genuine ecological interest beyond typical recreational beaches.

Praia do Gunga succeeds because it offers rare geographic drama—where two water systems meet—while maintaining authentic beach culture and accessible adventure experiences rooted in regional tradition.
