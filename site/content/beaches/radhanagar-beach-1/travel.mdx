# Radhanagar Beach Travel Guide

Radhanagar Beach is one of India's most celebrated tropical beaches, located on Havelock Island (now officially Swaraj Dweep) in the Andaman Islands. The beach's isolation in the Arabian Sea, combined with its distinctive powder-white sand and tropical setting, makes it a genuine destination rather than a casual day trip. Reaching Radhanagar requires intentional planning—but that distance is precisely what preserves the beach's character.

## Getting There

### Arriving at Port Blair

The gateway to the Andaman Islands is **Veer Savarkar International Airport (IXZ)**, located in Port Blair, approximately 45 kilometers from Radhanagar Beach. IXZ handles flights from major Indian cities including Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, and Kolkata. Flight duration from Delhi is approximately 3.5 hours; from Chennai, 2.5 hours.

From IXZ, most visitors spend at least one night in Port Blair before heading to Havelock Island. This overnight stay allows for flight connections and acclimatization to the island climate.

### Ferry from Port Blair to Havelock Island

The primary route to Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep) is by ferry from Port Blair's harbor. Two ferry operators serve this route:

**Makruzz**: Runs high-speed ferries from Port Blair to Havelock jetty, journey time approximately 1.5–2 hours. Boats depart in early morning (typically 6:30–7:00 AM) and early evening (4:00–4:30 PM). Fares range from 400–600 INR ($5–7 USD) depending on boat class.

**Green Ocean & Nautika**: Alternative operators offering similar schedules and pricing. Green Ocean occasionally runs additional daytime departures.

Ferry schedules vary seasonally; during monsoon months (June–September), services may be reduced or cancelled due to rough seas. Always verify schedules and sea conditions before booking.

### Island Transportation to Radhanagar

Once at Havelock Island's main jetty, Radhanagar Beach is located approximately 12 kilometers across the island. Transportation options include:

**Scooter/Motorcycle rental**: Available at the jetty for 300–400 INR/day ($3.50–5 USD). The road to Radhanagar is paved and straightforward. Journey time is 20–30 minutes depending on traffic and road conditions.

**Taxi**: Available at the jetty. A taxi to Radhanagar costs 400–600 INR ($5–7 USD) one-way. Drivers typically wait while you visit and return you to the jetty.

**Shuttle services**: Some resorts offer shuttle services from the jetty; arrange with your accommodation in advance.

**Shared jeeps**: During high season, informal shared jeep services operate between the jetty and popular beaches, typically 150–200 INR per person.

### Recommended Itinerary

- **Day 1**: Arrive IXZ in Port Blair. Spend night in Port Blair or travel to Havelock if arriving early enough.
- **Day 2**: Take morning ferry to Havelock. Settle accommodation. Afternoon/evening visit to Radhanagar for sunset viewing.
- **Day 3**: Full day at Radhanagar and nearby beaches (Elephant Beach, Kalapathar).
- **Day 4**: Return ferry to Port Blair or explore other Havelock sites.

## Best Time to Visit

### Seasonal Overview

**October–April** is the dry season and the only time Radhanagar is reliably accessible and swimmable. Peak season runs November–February with favorable weather, calm seas, and reliable ferry schedules.

**October–November**: Pleasant weather, manageable crowds, water conditions stabilizing after monsoon. Temperatures 25–30°C (77–86°F).

**December–February**: Peak season. Coolest temperatures (23–28°C / 73–82°F), lowest rainfall, best visibility for water activities. Turtle nesting occurs during this window, particularly January–February.

**March–April**: Warming temperatures (28–32°C / 82–90°F), increasing humidity, but still acceptable conditions. Turtle nesting tapers.

**May–September**: Monsoon months. Heavy rainfall, rough seas, limited ferry schedules, reduced visibility. The beach is often inaccessible or restricted. Many tourist facilities reduce operations. Unless you have flexibility, avoid this period.

## Where to Stay

Radhanagar Beach itself has no hotels directly on the sand; it functions as a day-use beach. Accommodation is located on Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep), primarily in the main settlement area near the jetty, 12 kilometers from Radhanagar.

### Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep)

The island's primary accommodation cluster is near the jetty and spreads across the island interior. Options range from basic guesthouses to upscale resorts.

**Budget guesthouses**: 1,500–2,500 INR/night ($18–30 USD). Basic rooms, shared bathrooms, minimal amenities. Run by local families or small operators.

**Mid-range hotels and resorts**: 3,000–6,000 INR/night ($36–72 USD). Private bathrooms, basic AC or fan, on-site restaurants, tour desk services.

**Upscale resorts**: 8,000–15,000+ INR/night ($96–180+ USD). AC, private baths, good dining, organized tours, often beachfront or beachside on other islands.

**Homestays and vacation rentals**: 2,500–5,000 INR/night ($30–60 USD). Variable quality; common and increasingly popular.

Many visitors book accommodations on Havelock Island and day-trip to Radhanagar, visiting in late afternoon to catch sunset. This strategy is practical and economical.

### Port Blair

If arriving IXZ late or requiring flexibility, Port Blair offers diverse accommodation: 2,000–10,000+ INR/night depending on category. Spend one night here and ferry to Havelock in the morning.

## Costs

### General Price Level

Radhanagar Beach itself is **free to access**. There is no entrance fee or day-use charge.

### Transportation Costs

- **Domestic flight to Port Blair (IXZ)**: 4,000–12,000 INR ($48–145 USD) from major Indian cities, depending on advance booking.
- **Ferry (Port Blair to Havelock)**: 400–600 INR ($5–7 USD) one-way.
- **Island transport to Radhanagar**: 300–600 INR ($3.50–7 USD) depending on method (scooter, taxi, or shuttle).

### Accommodation Costs

- **Havelock Island**: 1,500–15,000+ INR/night ($18–180+ USD) depending on category.
- **Port Blair**: 2,000–10,000 INR/night ($24–120 USD).

### Food and Activities

- **Meals**: 300–800 INR ($3.50–10 USD) for casual meals; 800–2,000+ INR ($10–24+ USD) for mid-range restaurants.
- **Organized tours** (snorkeling, island hopping, scuba): 2,500–5,000 INR ($30–60 USD) per activity.
- **Water activity rentals** (rarely needed at Radhanagar): 500–1,500 INR ($6–18 USD).

### Overall Daily Budget

Budget approximately 3,500–6,000 INR ($42–72 USD) per day for accommodation, meals, and local transport on Havelock Island. Add 400–600 INR ($5–7 USD) for ferry costs and 300–600 INR ($3.50–7 USD) for transport to the beach.

**Total for multi-day trip**: Plan 15,000–25,000 INR ($180–300 USD) per person for a 4-day Havelock Island trip including accommodation, meals, ferry, and local transport.

## Safety

### Water Conditions and Swimming

Radhanagar's water is generally safe for swimming during designated daylight hours (typically 8 AM–5 PM) during the October–April season. However:

- **Saltwater crocodile incident (2010)**: A fatal attack in 2010 prompted the establishment of monitored swim zones. Lifeguards now monitor the beach during peak season (December–February). Designated swim areas are marked with flags.

- **Respect flagged zones**: Swimming is permitted only in designated areas marked by lifeguards. Do not venture beyond marked zones. Crocodile sightings are rare, but the 2010 incident demonstrates the real risk.

- **Water temperature**: Tropical year-round (26–29°C / 79–84°F). Wetsuits are unnecessary but sun protection is essential.

- **Currents and visibility**: Bay of Bengal currents are generally moderate. Visibility is excellent during calm conditions but can reduce after rain. Avoid swimming during or immediately after heavy rain.

- **No dangerous sharks**: Zero recorded shark incidents at this beach. Snorkeling and water activities are safe with standard precautions.

Swim during daylight, with the buddy system, and only in designated areas. Avoid swimming alone or after sunset.

### General Beach Safety

- **Valuables**: Don't leave unattended belongings on the beach. Use lockers at your accommodation.
- **Sun exposure**: UV index is high. Use high-SPF sunscreen and reapply frequently. Sunburns are common and debilitating.
- **Dehydration**: Carry adequate water. Tropical heat is deceptive.
- **Marine life**: Avoid touching coral or shells. Stonefish and sea urchins inhabit rocky areas; wear water shoes if exploring tide pools.

### Health Considerations

- **Water quality**: Monitored by local authorities. Generally safe during the October–April season.
- **Vaccinations**: Standard travel vaccinations recommended for India (typhoid, hepatitis A/B, tetanus).
- **Mosquito-borne illnesses**: Dengue and malaria are present in the Andaman Islands. Use insect repellent, particularly at dusk.
- **Medical facilities**: Port Blair has adequate hospitals and clinics. Havelock Island has basic medical facilities. For serious incidents, evacuation to Port Blair may be necessary.

## Local Tips

**Arrive early for sunset.** Radhanagar's western orientation makes it a prime sunset location. The best light occurs 45–60 minutes before sunset. Arrive by mid-afternoon to secure a good viewing spot, particularly in peak season.

**Respect turtle nesting closure signage.** During December–April, portions of the beach may be cordoned off for turtle nesting monitoring. Do not enter closed areas. Nesting is a privilege to witness, not exploit.

**Bring adequate sun protection.** The tropical sun is intense. High-SPF sunscreen, a hat, and beach cover-up are non-negotiable. Sunburns here are severe and spoil multi-day trips.

**Visit Elephant Beach and Kalapathar.** Havelock Island has other notable beaches worth visiting: Elephant Beach (good for snorkeling, 20 minutes north) and Kalapathar (dramatic sunrise from black sand beach, worth a pre-dawn visit).

**Hire local guides for island exploration.** Scooter rentals provide independence, but hiring a local taxi driver for a full-day island tour (1,500–2,500 INR / $18–30) provides cultural context and navigational ease.

**Plan ferry timing carefully.** Morning ferries fill quickly in peak season. If you're day-tripping from Port Blair, take the earliest available ferry and return on the evening service. This maximizes beach time.

**Pack reef-safe sunscreen.** The waters around Havelock are part of a marine ecosystem. Reef-safe sunscreen (zinc oxide or titanium dioxide based) protects coral.

**Expect limited WiFi and services.** Havelock Island is developing but remains less connected than mainland tourist hubs. Accommodation may have WiFi; the beach itself has no services.

**Book accommodation and ferries in advance during peak season.** November–February fills quickly. Book 2–4 weeks ahead if possible.

**Explore Havelock's other attractions.** The island offers Elephant Beach (snorkeling and mangrove kayaking), Kalapathar Beach (dramatic sunrise), Vijayananda Caves, and various forest trails. A multi-day stay is rewarded.

**Permits are simple.** As of 2022, foreign nationals visiting Andaman Islands do not require special permits for standard beach tourism. Entry is on arrival. This remains stable, but verify current policy before travel.
